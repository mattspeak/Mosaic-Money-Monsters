## 02 — Design Principles

What separates strategic insight from surface-level feedback.

---

## A). A clear bullseye audience unlocks better insight

Insight quality depends on a homogeneous audience: when participants share similar context, capability, and motivations, patterns become clear and actionable. When the audience is too broad, feedback averages out into "it depends" - and you lose decision clarity.

### ⚠️ Why 12–18 is too broad

*A 12-year-old and an 18-year-old differ materially in:*

**Development**
Cognitive development and attention patterns shift drastically.

**Context**
Maths confidence and real-world money context vary significantly.

**Autonomy**
Pocket money vs part-time work / imminent adulthood.

### Recommendation: Two Distinct Bands

**13-15 EARLY — Young Teens**
Forming habits, school-distributed adoption likely. This group needs strong clarity, scaffolding, and confidence-building.

**16-18 OLDER — Older Teens**
More "real money" relevance (jobs, bills). Higher scepticism and stronger views on what's "cringe" vs credible.

### STRATEGY

These bands need to be analysed separately to avoid "dirty averages" hiding the truth. Given budget/timeline constraints, we recommend selecting one band.

We sharpen hypotheses via expert review & AI simulation before engaging real users.

### Synthetic AI Insights
**SIMULATION & HYPOTHESES**

🌐 AI-driven simulation of how target users might respond to proposition and onboarding.

💬 Generates hypotheses on comprehension barriers and language that lands vs. alienates.

Contrast reveals preferences users can't articulate in isolation.

### ⭐ Best-in-Class Inspiration

## 1. Research Design & Preparation

Setting up for success

**Week 1**

### A — Research design & recruitment planning

- Confirm the bullseye audience, age bands, and screening criteria across UK and Czech markets
- Align on the core research questions and what "good" looks like for each objective
- Build a clear recruitment brief with quotas and safeguards (including parental consent)

### B — Discussion guide & stimulus development

- Develop the full moderation guide: warm-up, core tasks, and lightweight co-creation exercises
- Prepare competitor, alternative, and "do nothing" reference points for in-session comparison
- Create stretch stimulus to explore future directions and divergent options where helpful

### C — Expert review & synthetic insight optimisation

- Run a structured expert review of the proposition, drawing on our experience testing dozens of apps (including in FS and youth contexts)
- Identify obvious friction, missed best practice, and comprehension risks before fieldwork
- Use a synthetic AI insight layer to generate sharper hypotheses, stress-test language, and focus sessions on what only real users can validate

## Research Techniques

From usability fundamentals to deeper behavioural drivers.

### CORE FUNDAMENTALS

**👁️ Blink Testing**
Fast read on clarity, relevance, and memorability – mirrors real-world app discovery.

**🎙️ Think-Aloud**
Users narrate expectations as they navigate. Observing reality vs intention.

**❓ Comprehension**
"How would you explain this to a friend?" Understanding vs click-through.

**📊 Context & Usage**
Classroom, solo, with parents? Functional, social, and emotional jobs.

**⚖️ Forced Trade-Offs**
Expose real priorities by forcing choices. What's non-negotiable?

### DEEPER INSIGHTS

**📈 Progress Mapping**
Explores what "getting better with money" actually means to teens.

**🔴 JTBD Switch**
What triggers action vs inertia? Explores pushes, pulls, and anxieties.

**🔄 Novelty vs Habit**
Is this "one and done"? Probes repeat use, shareability, and fit.

**◐ Projective Prompts**
Uncover tone sensitivity & emotion via sentence completion.

**✎ Co-Creation**
"Pitch to a sceptical friend." "Write the TikTok caption."
