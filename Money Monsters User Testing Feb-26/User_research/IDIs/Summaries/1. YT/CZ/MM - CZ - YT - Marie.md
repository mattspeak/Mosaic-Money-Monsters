# ✅ R01 YT 12-14 \- Marie, YT, 14

## **Interview 1 Summary**

**Segment:** Younger Teen (12–14)
female, 14

Participant is financially somewhat aware but still structurally dependent on parents. She shows strong engagement with money in daily life (especially spending), but has limited autonomy in banking and online transactions.

## **Relationship with money**

* Has a bank account with parents but no independent access to it.
  Primarily uses cash.
* When traveling or shopping, mother either gives her card temporarily or gives her cash.
* Shops frequently and enjoys shopping.
* Tries to actively divide money received (e.g., for anniversary) into:
  * 50% spending
  * 50% saving
* Shows early budgeting instinct.

### **Role of Family**

* Father explains online shopping risks and scams.
* Mother explains daily spending.
* Parents handle online purchases for her.
* Not used to buying online independently.

### **Emotional Baseline**

* Feels relatively comfortable with money.
* However, explicitly acknowledges knowledge gaps:
  * Online shopping
  * How banks work
  * Scams
* Aware that she lacks information.

**From propositions (ranked preference):**

1. **"Money stuff nobody explains" (Proposition 3\)** – strongest resonance
2. "Real Money Skills for Life"
3. "Train Your Money Brain"

**Why Proposition 3 resonated:**

* Wants content adapted to her needs and language.
* Interested in practical explanations.
* Wants to understand how to operate with money better.
* Feels there are gaps no one has clearly explained.

**App Experience**

**First Impression**

* Compared it to Duolingo (positive reference).
* Strong positive reaction to monsters and visual identity.
* Liked Mr. Korunka / Mr. Money as guide.

**Usability & Comprehension**

* Quiz questions were clear and understandable.
* Navigated without difficulty.
* No confusion observed.
* Understood structure and levels.

**Learning Experience**

* Appreciated explanations after mistakes.
* Valued ability to retry.
* Even when she already knew something, explanations helped:
  * Summarize
  * Reinforce
  * Remind

### **Gamification Response**

Very strong positive response:

* Loved monsters.
* Motivated by streak / day tracking.
* Liked progressing through levels.
* Found the structure motivating for return use.
* Understood topics clearly.

**Content Fit & Expectations**

* Would prefer clear separation between:
  * Beginner content
  * More advanced content
* Feels confident in some areas but not others.
* Wants adaptive content based on level.
* Interested in choosing topics where she lacks confidence.

### **Standalone vs Embedded**

* Does not use bank app yet.
* Would prefer integration into School system (Bakaláři)
* Reason:
  * Already uses it.
  * Feels natural.

* Alternatively open to separate app.
* Embedded in banking app not relevant to her current life stage.

## **Overall segment signal**

* Is spending-oriented but financially somewhat conscious.
* Shows early saving discipline.
* Has strong interest in practical, clearly explained content.
* Highly responsive to gamification.
* Still structurally dependent on parents for financial operations.
* Motivated by progress tracking and visual rewards.
* Would benefit from tiered difficulty structure.
