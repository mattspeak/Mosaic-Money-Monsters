# ✅ R18 YT 12-14 \- Lily, YT, 12

## **Interview 18 Summary**

Segment: Younger Teen (12–14)
Female, Lily, 12 years old

Participant recently turned 12 and is already beginning to think about financial independence. She shows curiosity about money management and expresses a strong desire to earn her own money in the future rather than rely on parents.

**Relationship with money**

* Has bank accounts at **Komerční banka** and **Česká spořitelna** and uses the banking apps (mostly KB) to monitor her balance.
* Currently saving for a **new phone**, which she estimates will take about **one year** to afford.
* In the past she and her brother **saved money together for a Nintendo**, which she described as a positive experience.
* She is motivated to save, but doesn't have any strategy
* She already thinks about financial independence, explaining that she **does not like asking parents for money** and is looking forward to getting a **part-time job when she turns 16**.

**Self-assessment of financial knowledge**

* Rated her financial literacy **6/10**.
* She described this as neutral – she feels she understands some things but still has a lot to learn.

**Sources of financial information**

* She mainly learns about finances **independently through online videos and internet content**.
* Currently attends **fifth grade**, and financial literacy is **not yet part of her school curriculum**, although she expressed interest in learning more about money at school.

**Proposition preference**

* Preferred proposition: **"Real Money Skills for Life."**
* She interpreted it as learning **practical things she can actually use in real life**.

**App experience**

**First impression**

* She described the app as **fun and engaging**, although she suggested it could include **more colors** to make it visually richer.
* She found the onboarding **clear and easy to understand**.
* She described **Mr. Korunka** as friendly and sympathetic.

**Learning experience**

* She enjoyed the lessons and found them **playful and engaging**.
* She mentioned that she **learned several new things**, for example realizing that she does not know how to transfer money into a savings account.

**Content fit and expectations**

* She suggested the app should include **a section explaining financial terms**, where users could browse and learn the meaning of unfamiliar concepts.
* She mentioned that many financial terms used in the lessons were **new to her**.

**Gamification and engagement**

* She responded very positively to the idea of **collecting items and rewards**.
* She suggested features such as:
  * collecting **flowers or other items**
  * earning **points or coins**
  * using rewards to **buy items for characters**.
* She also liked the idea of having **a profile where she could take care of the monster characters**, similar to nurturing a character over time.
* She expressed interest in:
* collecting monsters
* earning points
* unlocking items for characters.

**Standalone vs embedded**

* She would prefer the app to be **a standalone application**.

**Monetisation expectations**

* She does not think **paid features are appropriate**, explaining that children usually **do not have their own money to spend on apps**.

**Overall rating**

* Rated the app **10/10**.
* She described it as:
* very practical
* fun to use
* educational.
* She said she learned a lot and enjoyed the playful format.

**Additional observations**

* She was the **only participant who did not spontaneously compare the app to Duolingo**.
* When asked directly, she said she knows Duolingo but **does not enjoy using it**, partly because the explanations were not clear enough for her.
* She perceived this app as **more playful and easier to understand**, which made the learning experience more enjoyable.

**Overall segment signal (individual level)**

* she shows early motivation to become **financially independent**
* enjoys **interactive and playful learning environments**
* values **clear explanations of financial terminology**
* is highly responsive to **gamification, collection mechanics, and character progression**
* expects educational apps for her age group to remain **free of paid content**
* perceives the app as both **fun and useful for learning about money**.
