# ✅ R03 YT 12-14 \- Ladislav, YT, 13

## **Interview 3 Summary**

Segment: Younger Teen (12–14)
Male, 13 years old

Participant shows interest in financial basics. He already has access to a banking app and engages with it occasionally. However, he is at an early stage of saving and long-term financial planning.

**Relationship with money**

* Has a bank account in mBank with access via mobile app.
* Checks overview of spending from time to time.
* Would like clear overview of monthly balance and spending categories.
* Used to pay by mobile (card added to phone).
* Currently requesting a physical card.
* Earns some money from refereeing football matches (cash).
* Spends mostly in cash.
* Does not use a wallet — keeps cash in a drawer/locker at home.

Spends mainly on:
– Food
– PlayStation games
– Small leisure purchases (e.g., minigolf set he later regretted buying)

* Recently started saving for the first time.
* Saving for a new iPhone.
* Estimates it will take approximately one year to save enough.

**Role of family and school**

* Does not speak much about money with family.
* With friends, discusses money only when contributing to shared purchases.
* Has Financial Literacy course at school and finds it interesting.
* Actively asks teacher additional questions when something is unclear.
* Does not proactively search for financial information (except practical needs such as ATM location).
* Shows general curiosity and openness to learning financial basics.

**Emotional baseline**

* Sees himself as somewhat knowledgeable (self-assessed above beginner level – but imho he know less than he thinks he knows).
* Interested in improving understanding.
* Some previous experiences of pretending to understand when unsure.

**Proposition preference (ranked)**

"Real Money Skills for Life" – strongest resonance

Other propositions less compelling

Why Proposition 2 resonated ("Real Money Skills for Life" – strongest resonance)

* Perceived as relevant to his life.
* Interested in:

  – Scams

  – Making money last longer

  – Practical money management

**App experience**

**First impression**

* Strongly compared it to Duolingo (positive reference).
* Also compared topic/monster system to Pokémon Go.
* Mixed reaction to design — described as "too childish" multiple times.
* Perceives himself as older than the target suggested by the visual style.


**Usability and comprehension**

* Expected quizzes about finances — expectation fulfilled.
* Liked presence of back button.
* Understood onboarding quiz as diagnostic ("poznávací").
* Forgot about Mr. Korunka; guide character did not leave strong impression.

**Learning experience**

* Some content felt easy; some felt difficult.
* Appreciated explanations after mistakes.
* Positive reaction while completing lessons.
* Found lesson about euro particularly interesting.
* Noticed progressive difficulty and appreciated that.

**Suggested improvements:**
– Less chat-style content
– More quiz questions
– Possibility for multiple correct answers in certain scenarios
– Option to skip easier questions and start at higher difficulty level

Self-assessed knowledge level: approximately 3.5 out of 5, while onboarding level felt like 2 out of 5\.

**Gamification and design response**

* Recognizes motivational potential of streak system.
* Would be motivated by daily usage similar to Duolingo.
* However, monsters and overall design perceived as too childish.
* Would prefer design adjusted for slightly older audience (more mature visual tone).

**Content fit and expectations**

* Understood topic overview.
* Interested mainly in practical money management topics relevant to his current situation.
* Some topics felt less relevant.
* Would like app to be more clearly targeted at his age group.

**Standalone vs embedded**

* Believes app could be useful in school context.
* Could imagine it being recommended by financial literacy teachers.
* In current design, more suitable for younger students.
* If redesigned for older teens, he could imagine using it regularly.

**Monetisation expectations**

* Strongly believes app should be free.
* Sees paid features as unfair to people with different economic backgrounds.
* Would not want to pay for access.
* He can imaging earning coins for inapp activities and then spending it on app features. But he is against paying for anything (he said he buys something on Roblox or PS once per year only)

**Overall segment signal**

* This participant is financially curious.
* Has early exposure to digital banking tools.
* Values practical, real-life financial skills.
* Responds positively to structured progression and quiz-based learning.
* Sensitive to perceived age positioning — design maturity is critical.
* Would engage more if difficulty adapts to his knowledge level.
* Strong resistance to monetisation.
