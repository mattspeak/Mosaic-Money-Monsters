# ✅ R15 YT 12-14 \- Franta, YT, 13

## **Interview 15 Summary**

Segment: Younger Teen (12–14)
Male, 13 years old

Participant already has some basic experience managing small amounts of money and shows early interest in saving and financial self-regulation. Although he recognizes that he still has much to learn about finances, he has developed simple strategies to control spending.

**Relationship with money**

* Has a **bank account at Česká spořitelna** and uses both **card payments and cash**.
* Receives **pocket money from parents** and also receives money from relatives on birthdays.
* Keeps part of his money in **cash envelopes for saving**, currently saving for:
  * a **better helmet**
  * a **PlayStation steering wheel**.
* He has developed a personal saving strategy: from his pocket money he **always puts 100 CZK aside into a piggy bank**.
* He has also set a **personal spending limit of 300 CZK** for impulse purchases or "unnecessary things".

**Self-assessment of financial knowledge**

* Rated his financial literacy **5/10**.
* He explained that he believes he still has a lot to learn and that there are financial topics he does not yet understand.

**Sources of financial information**

* parents

**Proposition preference**

* Preferred proposition: **"Train Your Money Brain."**
* He found the idea interesting, although he mentioned that the wording sounded **slightly embarrassing or awkward** to him.

**App experience**

**First impression**

* He described the app as **visually appealing and similar to Duolingo**.
* He understood it as an application that teaches people **how to manage money in a fun way**.
* He expected the app to include **game-like elements and activities**.

**Usability and comprehension**

* He understood the purpose of the onboarding questions and recognized that they help identify **what the user needs to improve**.
* The difficulty level felt **appropriate**.

**Learning experience**

* He appreciated the **explanations after answers**, which helped clarify why certain answers were correct or incorrect.
* He liked the possibility of **making mistakes and learning from them**.

**Design response**

* He liked **Mr. Korunka**, describing him as a friendly character who explains things clearly.
* He also liked the monster characters.
* However, he felt the overall design looked **too childish**, as if the app were intended mainly for **children up to around 12 years old**.

**Content fit and expectations**

* Among the topics shown in the app, he was most interested in:
  * **saving money**
  * **future financial topics such as investing**.
* He mentioned that some topics (for example borrowing) do not yet feel relevant to him but could become useful **in the future**.

**Standalone vs embedded**

* He would prefer the app to be **integrated into a banking app**, so he would not need to download and manage another separate application.

**Monetisation expectations**

* He would prefer **not to pay for the app**.
* He mentioned that he would rather spend money on **a game or other products** than pay for additional features within the app.

**Overall rating**

* Rated the app **9/10**.
* He described it as:
* nicely designed
* educational
* a good way to learn about money.
* He also suggested that adding **more topics**, especially about **investing and future financial decisions**, could make the app even more useful.

**Overall segment signal (individual level)**

* already applies simple **self-control strategies for spending**
* shows early interest in **saving and financial planning**
* responds positively to **gamified learning formats**
* appreciates **clear explanations and the ability to learn through mistakes**
* perceives the visual design as **slightly too childish for his age**
* would prefer financial education tools to be **integrated with banking applications** rather than separate platforms.
