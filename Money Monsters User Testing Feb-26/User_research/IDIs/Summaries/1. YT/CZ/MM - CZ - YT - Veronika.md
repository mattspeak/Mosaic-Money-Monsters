# ✅ R06 YT 12-14 \- Veronika, YT, 13

## **Interview 6 Summary**

Segment: Younger Teen (12–14)
Female, 13 years old

The participant is a 13-year-old girl who is highly motivated by a specific long-term goal (studying in the USA). While she has some financial awareness regarding saving, she lacks foundational knowledge of banking mechanisms like interest rates and transaction fees.

**Relationship with Money**

* Income Source: She receives a monthly allowance (pocket money) from her parents.
* Saving Habits: She is currently saving all her money for a future study trip to America to help her parents with the costs.
* Banking Status: She keeps all her money in a standard current account and uses a mobile banking app.
* Spending: She has not made any large purchases recently, as she is strictly focused on her savings goal.

**Role of Family**

* Communication: She does not talk about money with her parents very often.
* Education: Her parents have taught her the basics, specifically advising her to save more and avoid spending on "nonsense".

**Emotional Baseline & Self-Perception**

* Peer Comparison: When comparing herself to her classmates on a scale of 1–10, she rates herself at a 6 or 7\.
* Knowledge Gaps: She admitted to not knowing how a savings account works or what "interest" actually entails until using the app.
* Attitude: She feels neutral about her financial situation; it doesn't worry her, but she isn't deeply engaged with it yet.

**From Propositions**

* "Real Money Skills for Life": This was the most attractive to her because it felt practical.
* "Train Your Money Brain": She disliked this one because it sounded like "testing" or a school exam.
* "Money Stuff Nobody Explains": This felt like it was for someone older than her.

**App Experience**

**First Impression & Design**

* Visual Identity: She found the design colorful and nice, but "too childish".
* Target Audience: Throughout the session, she repeatedly mentioned that the design felt like it was for younger children. She would prefer something more "serious".
* Navigation: She compared the interface to Duolingo and expected a similar step-by-step progression.

**Usability & Comprehension**

* Quizzes: She found the initial tests fine, though she hesitated and thought deeply about several questions where she lacked the underlying knowledge (e.g., interest rates).
* Guide/Mascot: She liked "Mr. Korunka" but suggested that users should be able to choose their own guide (e.g., a boy or a girl) to make it more personal.

**Learning Experience**

* The app successfully taught her two key concepts:
  1. The definition of interest and why a savings account is better than a current account for long-term goals.
  2. The fact that bank card transactions can involve hidden fees.

**Gamification Response**

* Streaks: She is highly motivated by "Streaks" (citing her experience with Duolingo).
* Feature Request: She strongly suggested a "Freeze" option for the streak so she wouldn't lose her progress if she missed a day, noting she might not return if a long streak was broken.
* Monsters/Gallery: She understood the collection aspect but only resonated with the "Saving" monster. She felt the "Loan" monster wasn't relevant to her life.

**Standalone vs. Embedded**

* Integration: She would prefer the tool to be integrated directly into her banking app so she could learn a concept and immediately apply it to her real money.
* Monetization: She is strongly against "Pay-to-Play" models. She believes payment should never influence the gameplay or progress.

**Overall Segment Signal**

* The participant is a goal-oriented saver who values practical utility over "gamey" aesthetics.
* While she responds well to Duolingo-style mechanics (streaks, levels), she finds the current visual style too juvenile for a 13-year-old.
* Her primary motivation is the transition from "saving in a piggy bank" (symbolic) to understanding real-world banking tools that help her reach her goal of going to America.
