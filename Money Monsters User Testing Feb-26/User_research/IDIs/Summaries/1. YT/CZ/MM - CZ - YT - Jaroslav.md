# ✅ R05 YT 12-14 \- Jaroslav, YT, 14

## **Interview 5 Summary**

Segment: Younger Teen (12–14)
Male, 14 years old

The participant is a 14-year-old student in the 8th grade. He is proactive about his finances, highly motivated by specific savings goals, and possesses a solid foundational understanding of banking and interest. He demonstrates a high level of digital literacy and an interest in gamified learning.

**Relationship with Money**

* Savings Oriented: He is highly disciplined with money, currently saving all his pocket money for a plane ticket to Korea.
* Banking Habits: He has had a savings account with a major Czech bank for approximately two years and actively uses their mobile app.
* Cash Management: When he needs physical cash, he transfers money from his account to his mother's account, and she gives him the equivalent in cash.
* Self-Assessment: He rates his financial literacy as a 7/10 compared to his peers, primarily because he saves significantly more than others who tend to spend their money.

**Role of Family & School**

* Family Guidance: His parents initiated his financial education two years ago when they set up his account. Currently, they focus on discussing saving strategies with him.
* School Gap: He has not yet had any financial literacy subjects in school; this is scheduled for the 9th grade.
* Key Concepts Learned: He specifically recalls learning about interest rates on his savings account from his parents.

**Emotional Baseline**

* Security Concerns: He chose the "clenched teeth" emoji to describe his feelings about money.
* Past Trauma: This anxiety stems from a past experience where his wallet was stolen by a pickpocket, making him fearful of losing his money again.

**From Propositions (Ranked Preference)**

* "Train Your Money Brain": This was his top choice, even though he admitted the phrasing felt a bit "cringe" or awkward.
* "Tell apart good and bad financial decisions" resonated well in connection to his spending for upgrades in video games (buy now or wait for discount).
* "Make Mistakes Safely Without Real Consequences": He found the idea of a "sandbox" or game-like environment for money interesting, though his reaction was less enthusiastic than for the first option.

**App Experience**

**First Impressions & Design**

* Duolingo Reference: He immediately compared the interface to Duolingo, which he viewed as a positive and familiar design choice.
* Visual Identity: He liked the design, the progression through levels, and the character Mr. Korunka.
* Monsters: He correctly identified that different financial topics were represented by different "monsters" and found this engaging.

**Usability & Learning Experience**

* Introductory Quiz: He found the questions to be at an appropriate difficulty level, though he wasn't entirely sure what the specific purpose of the initial quiz was.
* Interactivity: He suggested that instead of just multiple-choice questions, the app should occasionally require users to write in their own answers to prevent the experience from becoming repetitive.
* Positive Feedback: He reacted very positively to the "text message" style of communication within the lessons, noting it made the interaction feel more alive.

**Gamification & Motivation**

* Streak Power: He explicitly stated that a "streak" (daily tracking) would be the primary reason he would return to the app daily, similar to his current habit with Duolingo.
* In-App Economy: He independently suggested implementing an internal currency or coins earned through streaks that could be used to customize the Monsters or their houses.
* Customization: He proposed using these coins to buy upgrades, such as a "house" or environment for the monsters.
* Lives: He responded well to the idea of a "lives" system (recovering once a day) to add stakes to the learning process.

**Content Fit & Expectations**

* Interests: He is most interested in learning about saving and security/safety, directly reflecting his goal of going to Korea and his fear of theft (recent pickpocket experience).
* Decision Making: He expressed interest in a topic titled "Recognizing Good and Bad Financial Decisions," citing his own habit of buying in-game items for video games and waiting for sales.

**Standalone vs. Embedded**

* Standalone Preference: He would prefer the tool as a standalone app that he could place in a specific "Financial Literacy" folder on his phone or tablet.

**Overall Segment Signal**

* Score: 8/10: He gave the app a high rating, only deducting points because he wanted more varied interaction (like typing answers).
* High Engagement: He is a "power user" profile—digitally savvy, goal-oriented, and highly responsive to gamification mechanics like streaks and collections.
