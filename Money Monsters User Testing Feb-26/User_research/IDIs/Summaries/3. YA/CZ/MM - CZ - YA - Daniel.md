# ✅ R11 YA 18-25 \- Daniel, YA, 24

## **Interview 11 Summary**

Segment: Young Adult (18-25)
Male, 24 years old

The participant is a 24-year-old entrepreneur who is financially independent and highly self-reliant. He is motivated by financial stability and actively seeks out information to improve his financial literacy, though he finds existing educational resources lacking for his demographic.

**Relationship with Money**

* Financial Independence: He is a self-employed individual (OSVČ) who started working/business because he lacked the funds for further studies.
* Budgeting & Savings: His primary expenses are rent, bills, and food. He actively saves to maintain a "cushion" (buffer) equivalent to 6–7 months of expenses.
* Debt Management: Recently took out a business loan with a business partner. He approached this by conducting thorough research online and using AI tools before selecting a product from a reputable bank.

**Role of Knowledge & Information**

* Self-Taught: He prefers to find information himself rather than relying on others. He wants to manage his finances on his own to avoid fees for the service providers.
* Peer/Family Gaps: He mentioned that his parents have very different financial views than his own, and he feels his friends generally lack sufficient financial knowledge.
* Self-Assessment: He rates his financial literacy at a 6 or 7 out of 10, largely because he has practical experience with loans and business. But mentions that one can always be better and learn more.

**Emotional Baseline**

* Current State: He selected the "bicep" emoji to describe his current financial state, indicating he feels strong and capable.
* Priorities: His main drivers are ensuring he can handle his financial situation without "drowning" and avoiding traps like "buy now, pay later" schemes or hidden fees.

**From Propositions**

* "Money stuff nobody explains" (Proposition 3): This resonated most because he feels there are many "obvious" financial concepts that aren't actually explained well.
* "How to save and what for": Interested in optimizing his savings.
* "What to watch out for": Focused on risk mitigation and avoiding surprises.

**App Experience**

**First Impression**

* Negative/Childish: Initially thought the app was for children due to the "monster" avatars and visual style.
* Design Mismatch: He explicitly disliked the design and monsters.

**Usability & Comprehension**

* Onboarding: Compared the "Mr. Korunka" guide to a corporate Human Resources (HR) training.
* Difficulty: Found the questions and level of difficulty to be appropriate for his knowledge level. Understood the purpose of the onboarding.
* Interface: He compared it to scrolling in TikTok-style but while learning found the SMS/chat-style interaction positive and engaging.

**Learning Experience**

* New Insights: Despite his initial negative expectations/experience, he learned new things, such as the fact that mobile operators or energy providers can access credit registries.
* Issues: He struggled to remember specific names (e.g., distinguishing between characters Radek and Michal) during a lesson about AI investment (similar to R08)

**Gamification & Motivation**

* Streaks: This was the strongest motivator for him. He mentioned using a walking app with a 290-day streak and noted that the dopamine hit from maintaining a streak would keep him returning.
* Progression: He enjoyed the process of unlocking levels and monsters, despite his initial dislike of the visual style.
* Overall App Rating: 9/10 (Overall positive, despite visual critiques).

**Standalone vs. Embedded**

* Standalone: He can see it working as a dedicated financial education game.
* Banking Integration: If integrated into a banking app, he expects high synchronization—for example, seeing lessons tailored specifically to his actual savings data within the bank.

**Monetization**

* Knowledge over Cosmetics: He is willing to pay for "advanced" content (e.g., lessons on bonds, term deposits, or crypto) but is not willing to pay for in-game items (lives).

**Overall Segment Signal**

* Key Feedback: The participant represents a "proactive" user who values efficiency and practical knowledge.
* While he finds the "monsters" too childish, the core mechanics (streaks, interactive lessons, and high-value topics like debt and investing) are highly effective for his demographic.
