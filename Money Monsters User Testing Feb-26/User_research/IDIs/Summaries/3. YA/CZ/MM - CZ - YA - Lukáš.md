# ✅ R10 YA 18-25 \- Lukáš, YA, 19

## **Interview 10 Summary**

Segment: Young Adult (18-25)
Male, 19 years old, high-school student

Participant is relatively financially independent for his age and actively earns income through multiple part-time jobs. He shows an interest in financial topics, particularly investing, although his financial management is largely self-taught and not based on a clear strategy.

**Relationship with money**

* Currently finishing high school and plans to start university next year.
* Works regularly in different part-time jobs, including promotional work in shops and selling Nespresso products.
* Has been earning his own money for the past four to five years.
* Still receives some financial support from his parents but considers himself largely financially independent.

* Spends money on everyday living costs such as food.
* Actively saves and invests money, although without a clear long-term strategy.
* Recently saved money for a car, which took approximately two years.

**Emotional Baseline**

* Financial Literacy Self-Rating: 9-10 – feels relatively confident in his financial abilities. However, during the interview it became clear that some financial concepts are still unfamiliar to him (e.g., RPSN).
* Emoji/Stressors: Confidents (first emoji on the left with sunglasses). Shows willingness to learn more and expand his understanding of financial topics.
* Mentioned a psychological challenge related to spending money (and taking money from the savings). When transferring money from his savings account to his current account for everyday expenses, he experiences mental pressure and feels that using savings is "not right," even when spending on necessary things like food.

**Sources of financial information**

* Initially learned about finances through personal experience and mistakes.
* Did not receive financial education at school during earlier years.
* Gradually became interested in financial topics and started learning independently.

**Proposition preference (ranked)**

* "Train Your Money Brain" – strongest resonance
* Other propositions felt less relevant:
  * Blue one perceived as too basic
  * Pink perceived as somewhat "populist"
* Why "Train Your Money Brain" proposition resonated
  * Associated it with improving financial decision-making and learning through practice.
  * Feels that everyday micro-decisions about money are the most challenging aspect of financial management.

**App experience**

**First impression**

* Immediately compared the app to Duolingo.
* Perceived the design as playful and suitable for younger audiences (asked what "younger" means to him, he said 18-25 – he noticed that for older people it would need more "serious" design.
* If the app focused more strongly on investing or financial markets, he would expect a more formal design.
* He said it could be used in schools for younger age groups.
* As a learning game, however, he felt the design works well.

**Usability and comprehension**

* Understood the purpose of the onboarding quiz.
* Suggested onboarding should be more detailed to better adapt content to the user's knowledge and needs.
* Did not particularly notice Mr. Korunka, but had no objections to the character.

**Learning experience**

* Did not know what RPSN meant and answered incorrectly. Read the explanation and acknowledged that even though he feels confident in finances, he could still learn new things.
* Suggested that explanations of financial terms such as RPSN or financial funds would be helpful.
* Also expects personalization of learning topics based on onboarding responses.

**Content fit**

* Would prefer a clearer structure of topics:
  * Larger thematic areas
  * Subtopics or chapters within each topic
* Suggested the possibility of an AI companion within the app that could answer questions about financial topics.
* Referenced an AI feature used by ČSOB bank as a positive example.

**Standalone vs embedded**

* He noticed that it would definitely be interesting if it were separate, but he leaned toward some kind of collaboration with a bank (adding an education section)

**Monetisation expectations**

* Believes it is normal for apps like this to require payment.
* Open to paying if the app offers high quality content and design.
* Would expect strong value if the app is paid.


**Overall segment signal**

* This participant is financially active and curious, with experience in earning, saving, and investing money (even though he still lacks a strategy).
* His financial knowledge is largely self-developed and experience-based rather than structured.
* Even confident users may still lack understanding of some financial concepts.
* Shows interest in deeper financial topics such as investing and financial products.
* Would benefit from personalized learning paths, allowing him to deepen what he already knows as well as learn new things.
* Open to paid solutions if they provide strong value and high-quality design.
