# ✅ R14 YA 18-25 \- Anna, YA, 21

## **Interview 14 Summary**

Segment: Young Adult (18-25)

Female, 21 years old

Participant shows interest in learning about financial topics and is open to educational tools that help build practical knowledge. She responded positively to the interactive and gamified structure of the app and expressed curiosity about learning more through the platform.

**Relationship with money**

* She lives with her parents
* Looking for a job for the past 6 months
* Get money from parents
* Doesn't have any savings or saving goals
* Buys on sales (she has a Tesco Club Card)
* The participant showed general awareness of financial safety and the importance of understanding financial decisions.
* She expressed that an ideal financial app should help users recognize risks and warn them about potentially unsafe financial situations, providing guidance on how to respond.

**Emotional baseline**

* She appears generally comfortable discussing financial topics and curious about learning more.
* Her reactions during the session suggested that she views financial learning as something that can be engaging and exploratory rather than stressful.

**Proposition response**

* "Train Your Money Brain" – strongest resonance
* She responded positively to the general idea of learning practical financial skills through an interactive application.

**App experience**

**First impression**

* **She described the app as well organized and structured like a game.**
* The progression through levels reminded her of Duolingo, which she mentioned as a positive reference.
* She liked the monster characters, describing them as visually appealing.
* She perceived **Mr. Korunka** as more trustworthy and credible than the monsters because he appears more intelligent and professional.
* She found the onboarding **well designed and easy to understand**.
* The structure of lessons encouraged her to **continue learning and progress further**.

**Learning experience**

* She appreciated that:
  * mistakes were allowed
  * explanations were provided after incorrect answers.
* She described the experience as **"trial and error" learning**, which she found engaging.
* The questions felt **somewhat challenging**, which she perceived positively.

**Gamification and motivation**

* She mentioned that **rewards and achievements** would motivate her to return to the app.
* Receiving a **trophy after completing a lesson** created a strong positive feeling for her.
* She appreciated the sense of progress and accomplishment.

**Content fit and expectations**

* She suggested that **explanations could be improved**, particularly for questions she answered incorrectly.
* She would appreciate:
* clearer explanations
* more understandable descriptions of financial concepts.

**Standalone vs embedded**

* She would prefer the app to be **a standalone application**, separate from banking apps.
* She explained that she would prefer **not to mix financial education with her personal banking tools**.

**Monetisation expectations**

* She has experience from buying things in MainCraft
* She does **not want advertisements** within the app.
* She was hesitant to pay for extra content

Overall rating

* She rated the app **8–9 out of 10**.
* She liked:
* the overall style
* the gamified structure
* the opportunity to learn new things.

**Overall segment signal (individual level)**

* The app was perceived as both **engaging and educational**, with strong potential to support ongoing learning about financial topics.
* she responds positively to **gamified learning formats**
* she appreciates the ability to **learn through experimentation and mistakes**
* she values **clear explanations of financial concepts**
* she prefers **financial education tools to remain separate from banking applications**
* she finds **rewards and achievements motivating for continued engagement**.
