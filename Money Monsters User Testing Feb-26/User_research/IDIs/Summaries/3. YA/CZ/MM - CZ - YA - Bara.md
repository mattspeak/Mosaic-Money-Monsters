# ✅ R02 YA 18-25 \- Bara, YA, 18

## **Interview 2 Summary**

Segment: Young Adult (18–25)

Female, 18 years old (high school student)

Participant is partially financially independent in terms of income but still early in financial maturity. She earns her own money through side jobs and manages her day-to-day expenses herself. She focuses mainly on present financial needs rather than long-term planning.

* **Relationship with money**
* Works two side jobs (Prague Castle and office cleaning with friends).
* Shares earned money with friends when working together.
* Does not receive additional financial support from parents.
* Uses mBank and actively uses rounding-up feature to transfer small amounts into savings.
* Spends mostly on:
   – Food (daily snacks, fast food with friends)
   – Gym
   – Minimal spending on clothes

Does not talk much about finances with family or friends. With friends, finances are discussed mainly in practical terms (earning money, saving for something specific).

Learns very little about finances at school.

Focused primarily on current, day-to-day financial needs.

* Expressed attitude that some financial topics will be dealt with "when the time comes."
* Does not actively think long-term about finances.
* Currently saving for a vacation with friends (trip to Nice, France).

**Proposition preference (ranked)**

"Money stuff nobody explains" – strongest resonance
 "Train Your Money Brain"
 "Real Money Skills for Life"

**Why Proposition 3 resonated**

* Feels there are financial topics that are not clearly explained.
* Interested in understanding practical, real-life aspects relevant to her situation.
* Prefers content that connects directly to her personal context rather than abstract concepts.

**App experience**

* Understood there would be a quiz.
* Initially expected knowledge-based testing (e.g., about investing), but positively reacted when it focused on her personal situation instead.
* Liked animation and Mr. Korunka.
* Perceived older guide character as more trustworthy.
* Prefers animated character over real-person imagery.
* Compared the app to Duolingo.

**Usability and comprehension**

* Generally understood navigation and structure.
* During lesson, did not understand what RPSN meant.
* Would independently search Google or AI (ChatGPT) to clarify unknown terms.
* Missed clearer explanation of financial abbreviations (e.g., RPSN).

**Learning experience**

* Realized she does not know many things.
* Felt she learned new information.
* Appreciated that lessons were not too long and not boring.
* Found explanations good and helpful.

**Gamification and design response**

* Liked light and non-serious tone.
* However, described design as slightly childish.
* Would prefer:
   – More animation
   – Visual feedback (e.g., confetti after correct answer)
   – More dynamic interface
* Described current version as slightly "mrtvé" (not dynamic enough).

**Content fit and expectations**

* Understood monster/topic overview clearly.
* Would like additional topic on investing.
* Suggested clearer explanations of financial shortcuts and terminology.
* Suggested more dynamic visual design elements.

**Standalone vs embedded**

* Open to standalone downloadable app.
* However, believes discoverability would be easier if integrated into a banking app.
* Would be more likely to engage regularly if visible within banking app.
* Stated that seeing it daily in bank app would motivate her to complete lessons.

**Monetisation expectations**

* Expects core app to be free.
* Open to paid features if there is clear added value.
* Would expect benefits such as:
   – Unlocking additional content
   – More advanced topics
   – Coins or rewards for paid tier

**Overall segment signal**

* This participant is financially independent in income but present-focused in mindset.
* Engages actively with day-to-day spending and saving.
* Does not proactively plan long-term finances.
* Responds positively to personalized and situation-based content.
* Values clarity of financial terminology
* Open to gamification, but expects slightly more mature and dynamic design.
* Would likely engage more if integrated into a banking environment.
