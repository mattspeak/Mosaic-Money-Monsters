# ✅ R07 YA 18-25 \- Jan, YA, 19

## **Interview 7 Summary**

Segment: Young Adult (18-25)
Male 19 years old

The participant is a student in his final year of high school (preparing for graduation next year). He is partially financially independent through part-time work but still lives with his parents, who serve as his primary financial advisors.

**Relationship with Money**

* Income: Earns approximately 5,000 CZK per month working part-time as a waiter or bartender.
* Saving Habits: Demonstrates high financial discipline; he consistently puts 20–25% of his earnings into a savings account, primarily to fund summer vacations.
* Spending: His main expenses are food and socializing with friends.
* Major Decisions: His most recent significant purchase was a pair of expensive football boots.
* Banking: Uses Air Bank with both a checking and a savings account.

**Role of Family & Education**

* Family: His father is his primary resource for financial questions, followed by his mother. He recently consulted his father on whether to keep his summer savings in a standard savings account or invest them in funds.
* School: Feels he learned very little about money in school and prefers to seek out information independently.
* Digital Sources: Follows financial content on YouTube and TikTok; however, he trusts YouTube more, specifically mentioning a Czech YouTuber focused on finance.

**Emotional Baseline & Literacy**

* Self-Assessment: Rates his financial literacy at a 7 or 8 out of 10\.
* Knowledge Gaps: Feels confident in basic management but acknowledges a significant lack of understanding regarding investing and long-term wealth building.
* Mindset: Represented by the "thinking" emoji; he is constantly weighing his options between immediate saving and long-term investing.

**From Propositions**

* "Money stuff nobody properly explained": This resonated the most as it addresses things taken for granted that teens don't actually understand.
* "How to get more out of your money": Strong interest in optimization and value.
* "What to save for and how": Directly relates to his current behavior.

**App Experience**

**First Impression & Design**

* Initial Reaction: Compared it to Duolingo but initially worried the "monster" theme might be for younger children.
* Visual Guide: Responded better to "Mr. Korunka" (Mr. Coin) than the monsters, as it felt more "trustworthy" and age-appropriate.

**Usability & Comprehension**

* Difficulty: Found the introduction questions and tasks to be at an appropriately level.
* Interaction: Liked the text-message style interface as it felt like a nice break from standard quizzes.
* Feature Request: Desired a way to ask follow-up questions or chat with an AI/expert when an answer (correct or incorrect) is given, to understand the "why" in more detail.
  * Missing explanation of financial terminology e.g., "RSPN"/"APR".

**Gamification & Motivation**

* Engagement: Understood the concept of unlocking monsters for different topics (e.g., investing vs. saving).
* Retention: Mentioned that a "streak" feature (similar to Duolingo) would motivate him to return daily.
* Rewards: Suggested that bonuses or tangible perks for regular use would increase his commitment.

**Standalone vs. Embedded**

* Integration: Open to both, but strongly favors integration into his existing banking app.
* Reasoning: If it is inside his bank's app, it gains immediate "trustworthiness" because it is associated with an institution he already trusts with his money.

**Monetization & Content**

* Willingness to Pay: Would not pay for the app as a whole. However, he would consider small "micro-payments" for advanced, specialized topics (like deep-dives into investing) if the price was low.
* Content Expansion: Suggested adding educational videos (similar to the YouTube content he watches) directly into the app.

**Overall Segment Signal**

* Profile: A disciplined saver who is transitioning from "saving for a goal" to "investing for the future".
* Key Driver: Trust and Credibility. He is wary of "get rich quick" scams and seeks authoritative but accessible advice.
* Product Fit: High potential for an investment-focused module. He wants the app to be a bridge between his current bank account and his future investment portfolio.
