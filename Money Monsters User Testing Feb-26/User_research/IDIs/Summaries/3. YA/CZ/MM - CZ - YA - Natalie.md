# ✅ R08 YA 18-25 \- Natalie, YA, 24

## **Interview 8 Summary**

Segment: Young Adult (18-25)
Female 24 years old

The participant is a recent university graduate in molecular biology currently on maternity/parental leave. She is financially constrained, living on a tight budget with her partner, and relies on external advice for major financial decisions.

**Relationship with Money**

* Current Income: Her primary income is from parental benefits, supplemented by her partner's salary.
* Budgeting: Their budget is very tight; after paying the mortgage and essentials, they sometimes save 1,000 CZK, but often save nothing at all.
* Tools: She uses a banking app to track finances but finds its automatic categorization inaccurate and often lumps expenses into "Other".
* Decision Making: For the mortgage, they relied heavily on a family friend who is a financial advisor.

**Emotional Baseline**

* Financial Literacy Self-Rating: She rates herself a 4 out of 10\.
* Emoji/Stressors: She feels a need to "grit her teeth" for the next few years due to their strained financial situation.
* Motivation: She attributes her low self-rating to her inability to save and wants more control over her money to reduce stress.

**From Propositions**

* "Money stuff nobody explains" – strongest resonance. Refers especially for benefits system rules and applications and to rules connected to mortgages (how much they can get with a certain income, etc).
* "Things taken as a given that no one explains".

**Why Proposition Resonated**

* Information Gaps: She felt she didn't get enough clear information about the mortgage process (income requirements, bank limits) or state benefits.
* Practicality: She is specifically interested in navigating parental leave benefits—what she is entitled to and how to apply for them.
* Hidden Costs: She wants to be warned about fees and conditions, such as those associated with state contributions to savings accounts.

**App Experience**

**First Impression**

* Comparison: She immediately compared the interface to Duolingo.
* She overall likes the visual style.
* Visuals: She liked the design and the "Mr. Korunka" (Mr. Money/Coin) character. Suggested that going further in the app she'd like to change it to one of the monsters.
* Onboarding: Found the difficulty level appropriate and understood the purpose of the onboarding process.

**Usability & Comprehension**

* Terminology Issues: She was confused by technical terms like RPN (likely RPSN \- APR) and Credit Registry.
* Feature Request: She expects these terms to be clickable for a definition or explanation within the app.

**Learning & Gamification**

* Motivation: She is highly motivated by the "streak" (flame icon, noticed it at the first impression) and the collection of monsters.
* Customization: Suggested that for longer-term use, it would be interesting to be able to customize or choose her own monster avatar.
* Content Fit: The most relevant topics for her right now are budgeting, saving, and investing (commodities, crypto, funds, stocks).
* Suggestion: Adding a social aspect – connecting with her friends motivates her and challenges to be the best (collect most points/complete tasks/…).
* Learning: she mentions multiple times that she is learning new information and also learning what are all the areas she doesn't know and probably should know.

**Standalone vs. Embedded**

* Preference: Standalone app.
* Reasons:
  * Anonymity: She likes that the bank doesn't necessarily know who she is or what she's learning.
  * Perception: She views the bank as a "serious" entity, whereas the app is "playful," and she prefers to keep those worlds separate.

**Monetization**

* Willingness to Pay: Generally low; she typically only uses trial versions of apps.
* Hard No: She would not pay for "lives" or typical gaming elements.
* Potential Yes: She might consider paying for deeper, expert content on specific financial topics.

**Overall Segment Signal**

* The participant represents a "life-transition" user who is highly motivated by immediate financial pressure.
* While she enjoys the Duolingo-style gamification, she requires the app to bridge the gap between "playful learning" and "serious financial jargon" by providing better definitions of complex terms.
