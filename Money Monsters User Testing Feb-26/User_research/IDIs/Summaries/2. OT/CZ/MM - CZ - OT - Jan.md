# ✅ R13 OT 15-17 \- Jan, OT, 16

## **Interview 9 Summary**

Segment: Older Teen (15-17)
Male, Jan, 16 years old

Participant is interested in sports and cars. He already has his own bank account and regularly uses banking apps to monitor his finances. He appears relatively confident in managing everyday spending and saving, although his understanding of financial concepts remains mostly basic.

**Relationship with money**

* Has a bank account at **ČSOB** and uses the banking app to track transactions and monitor his balance.
* In an ideal financial app he would like to see:
  * overview of **income and spending**
  * tracking of **expenses**
  * **savings overview**
* He would also like the app to include:
* a **chat feature**
* **financial recommendations**
* **notifications about potential scams**
* the possibility to **arrange services such as online insurance**.
* Receives **pocket money** and sometimes earns money through **household chores**.
* Previously saved money for a **gaming steering wheel**.
* He used a **piggy bank** for savings and still occasionally uses it.

**Self-assessment of financial knowledge**

* Rated his financial skills **9/10**.
* He explained this by saying that he believes he generally manages money well and **does not spend on unnecessary things**.

**Sources of financial information**

* Mainly learns about money from **family members**.
* He mentioned in particular:
* his **grandmother**, who works in the insurance sector
* his **uncle**, who also shares advice.
* Topics he would like to understand better include:
* **interest**
* **investing**.

**Emotional baseline**

* Feels comfortable discussing money and **does not feel stressed about financial topics**.

**Proposition preference**

* Preferred proposition: **"Real Money Skills for Life."**
* Why this proposition resonated
  * He associated it with **practical knowledge he could apply in real life**, particularly related to:
* recognizing scams
* saving money
* planning future purchases.

* He also mentioned long-term goals such as:
* saving for **a car**
* managing **transport costs**
* eventually saving for **housing and financial stability in the future**.

**App experience**

**First impression**

* He immediately compared the app to **Duolingo** and perceived it as a kind of **financial learning game**.
* He found the onboarding useful because it encouraged him to think about:
  * how much he spends
  * what he spends money on
  * his future goals.

**Usability and comprehension**

* Onboarding was clear and easy to understand.
* He felt the difficulty level of the questions was **appropriate**.
* The explanations provided after answers made sense and helped him learn something new.

**Design response**

* He initially described **Mr. Korunka** as looking slightly like a "fat banker," but later said the character looked more like a **serious professional figure wearing a tie**, which he associated with financial expertise.
* Overall design felt acceptable to him, although the **monster characters felt somewhat childish**.

**Content fit and expectations**

* Among the topics shown in the lesson overview, he was most interested in:
  * **budgeting**
  * **spending tracking**.
* He also considered **saving money** to be an important topic.

**Standalone vs embedded**

* He would prefer the app to be **integrated into a banking app**, so that financial education and personal finances would be accessible **in one place**.

**Monetisation expectations**

* He would prefer **not to have paid content** in the app.

**Overall rating**

* Rated the app **7/10**.
* He described it as useful and educational, but felt that some design elements — especially the **monster characters** — made the app feel **too childish** for his age.
* He suggested that instead of monsters, the app could feature **adult characters (for example a man or woman working on a computer)** to make it feel more appropriate for older users.

**Overall segment signal (individual level)**

* he already uses banking tools and actively monitors his finances
* he feels quite confident about managing everyday spending
* he is interested in practical financial topics such as saving and budgeting
* he values learning that connects to **long-term financial goals**
* he perceives the current visual style as **slightly too childish for his age group**
* he would prefer financial education tools to be **integrated with existing banking apps** rather than separate platforms.
