# ✅ R12 OT 15-17 \- Niky, OT, 15

## **Interview 12 Summary**

Segment: Young Adult (18-25)

Transgender, Niky, 15 years old

Participant is finishing primary school and preparing for high school admission. His main personal interest is photography. He already has exposure to banking tools but remains financially dependent on his parents. He shows curiosity about financial topics but finds traditional financial education too technical and disengaging.

**Relationship with money**

* Has child accounts in **Komerční banka and Česká spořitelna**, both linked to his parents' accounts.
* Has access to banking apps where he can **track transactions and check his balance**.
* Receives **1,000 CZK pocket money**.
* Currently does not earn money. Previously earned money through **participating in theatre**, which he described as motivating – doing something and receiving money in return felt rewarding.
* As a younger child he saved money in a **piggy bank**, without a specific goal.
* In an ideal financial app he would like:
  * transaction tracking
  * **analysis and overview of spending patterns**.
* He feels current banking apps show individual transactions but **lack clear summaries that help plan future spending**.
* Uses both **card and cash payments**.

**Sources of financial information**

* Parents talk openly about money with him.
* However, he sometimes feels **hesitant to ask questions when he does not understand something**, because he worries he should already know the answer.
* He tries to overcome this but still finds it uncomfortable.
* Main sources:
* parents
* school.
* He remembered one guest lecture with an architect who explained financial topics particularly well.
* With classmates, financial topics appear mostly as **casual comments rather than serious discussions**.
* He also sometimes watches **finance-related content on YouTube**.

**School financial education**

* He has **financial literacy lessons at school**, but finds them:
  * too technical
  * terminology-focused
  * boring.
* He would prefer financial education to be **more interactive and engaging**.

**Proposition preference**

* Preferred proposition: **"Real Money Skills for Life."**
* Why this proposition resonated
  * He perceived this framing as **more credible and realistic**.
* The proposition **"Money Stuff Nobody Explains"** felt less trustworthy to him – he compared it to the tone of online news articles and felt it sounded exaggerated.

**Content interest**

* The topic that interested him most was **how to recognize scams**.
* Even though he has not personally experienced scams, the topic captured his attention.

**App experience**

**First impression**

* Described the app as:
  * pleasant
  * simple
  * visually appealing.
* He liked the mascot and felt that characters help **create a stronger emotional connection with the app**.

**Usability and comprehension**

* Onboarding was **clear, simple, and easy to understand**.
* Explanations after answers were helpful and well explained.
* He would prefer **not to lose points for incorrect answers**, as this might feel discouraging.

**Learning experience**

* He expected quizzes to become **gradually more difficult over time**, which aligns with his expectations for learning progression.
* After finishing the first lesson, he expected that the next lessons would either:
  * introduce **new topics**, or
  * continue the **same topic at a higher level of difficulty**.
* He also appreciated having characters present in the lessons and would prefer **more narrative interaction with them**.

**Design response**

* He preferred **Mr. Korunka over the monster characters**, describing him as more trustworthy and professional.
* Mr. Korunka looked to him **like a banker**, which increased the perceived credibility of the app.
* However, he still appreciated the overall colorful visual style and compared it positively to **Duolingo**.

**Content fit and expectations**

* He would like the app to allow **deeper exploration of topics after completing lessons**. For example:
  * access to additional explanations
  * ability to search information within the app
  * a kind of **"notebook" where users can revisit explanations and notes**.
* He mentioned that if the app included a search function and deeper explanations, it could feel **more reliable than searching through AI or multiple websites**, because everything would be in one place.
* Topics that interested him most included:
  * **saving**
  * **spending analysis**.

**Gamification and engagement**

* He liked:
  * streaks
  * levels
  * collecting characters.
* He imagined completing **several lessons in a row** when interested in a topic.
* He compared the learning format to platforms like **Umíme to**, where educational content is delivered in small interactive segments.

**Standalone vs embedded**

* He would prefer the app to be **a standalone application**.

**Monetisation expectations**

* He would **not want paid content**.
* He understands that paid features might exist, but personally would prefer the app to remain free.
* He also strongly disliked the idea of **advertisements within the app**.

**Overall rating**

* Rated the app **9/10**.
* He described the app as:
  * useful
  * enjoyable

  * something he could imagine using in his free time.
* He even suggested that the app could become **a positive alternative to social media browsing**, such as scrolling Instagram.

**Overall segment signal (individual level)**

* The app's simple structure and explanations worked well for him, and he perceives it as both **educational and entertaining**, with strong potential for regular use.
* he already interacts with banking tools but still relies financially on parents
* is curious about financial topics but discouraged by overly technical education
* values **clear explanations and practical learning**
* responds positively to **interactive and character-based learning formats**
* prefers **credibility and trustworthiness in visual elements**
* wants **deeper access to information once a topic becomes interesting**.
