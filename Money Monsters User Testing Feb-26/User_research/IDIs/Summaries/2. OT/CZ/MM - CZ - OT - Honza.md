# ✅ R04 OT 15-17 \- Honza, OT, 16

## **Interview 4 Summary**

Segment: Older Teen (15–17)
Male, 16 years old

The participant is a financially disciplined student with a solid theoretical foundation from both school and his parents, who are economists. He maintains a part-time job and focuses on long-term saving goals, though his interest in financial education is passive rather than proactive.

**Relationship with Money**

* Income & Savings: Earns money through a part-time job and receives cash as gifts or for trips.
* Budgeting: Generally disciplined, aiming to save approximately 30% of his income.
* Recent Spending: Made a large, unplanned purchase (a new mobile phone) because his previous one stopped working.
* Long-term Goals: Currently saving for a car as he approaches 18\.
* Self-Perception: Rates himself as a 5/10 on a financial literacy scale, comparing himself to a peer who trades in cryptocurrencies.

**Role of Family & School**

* Educational Background: Gained basic financial knowledge from a specific school subject in the 7th or 8th grade.
  * topics such as interest, compound interest and risky behavior connected to loans were covered.
* Parental Influence: His parents are economists, providing him answers to questions about their work.
* Security Awareness: Highly values online security, partly because one of his teachers was a victim of an online scam. In an IT course they covered IT/financial security as well.

**Emotional Baseline**

* Attitude: Generally relaxed and "passive."
* Information Seeking: Does not actively seek out financial information; he only engages with it when he has a specific need.
* Emoji Check-in: Chose a "sleepy/chilled" emoji, reflecting that he isn't currently stressed by financial matters.

**Value Propositions & Card Sorting**

* Top Preferences:
  * "Real Money Skills for Life"
  * "Simple financial skills you can start using immediately"
* Resonance: He gravitated toward the proposition: "How to spot a scam before you fall for it."
* Reasoning: He prefers practical, actionable skills over theory. He wants to know how to protect his money and how to reach his specific savings goals (like the car).

**App Experience**

**First Impression & Onboarding**

* Visual Identity: Viewed the character "Mr. Korunka" (Mr. Money) as slightly "childish," though acceptable.
* Compared the style to Duolingo.
* Onboarding Clarity: Expressed confusion regarding the goal of onboarding; he felt the initial questions were too simple and didn't see the reason in the process.

**Usability and learning**

* Lesson Content: The 50/30/20 rule lesson was new and interesting to him, same as role of 72 for savings account. He considered some content new knew a lot.
* Learning from Errors: Highly valued the feature that explains why an answer was wrong, noting that the app teaches through mistakes.
* UI Elements: The "chat-style" message interface didn't trigger a strong reaction; he saw it as just another way to present a question.
* Correct answers stand out: Participants noted that the correct answers stood out while the incorrect were non-sense responses.

**Gamification Response**

* Streaks: Understood the concept (comparing it to Duolingo) but explicitly stated that streaks do not motivate him to return to an app.
* Rewards: Initially confused by the monster/trophy screen (unsure what he actually "won"), but understood the collection aspect better once he saw the overview screen.

**Content fit and expectations**

* The topics in Monsters library were clear except for "Prediction".
* Overall rating: we would not use the app because he does not actively seek out information regarding financial matters.

**Suggested improvements**

* Topic Autonomy: Liked the ability to skip ahead to relevant topics, such as "Foreign Currencies," because of an upcoming school trip abroad. Having an option for on-demand learning on top of the linear journey.

**Standalone vs. Embedded**

* Preference: Standalone App.
* Reasoning: He finds existing banking apps already too complicated and cluttered. He would prefer a dedicated space for learning.

**Monetization & Features**

* Willingness to Pay: Open to paying for advanced lessons or specialized financial education.
* Lives/Hearts: Disliked the "lives" mechanic (penalizing wrong answers). He believes education should not punish mistakes especially as the app is explaining the mistakes.
* AI Support: Suggested an AI-driven chat for financial advice rather than standard "online support" which he didn't understand.

**Overall Segment Signal**

* Goal-Oriented: Motivated by specific purchases (car) and practical threats (scams).
* Skeptic of "Fluff": Not moved by traditional gamification like streaks or "cute" characters; wants direct utility.
* Practical Learner: Values autonomy in choosing topics and learning from practical mistakes rather than a fixed linear path.
