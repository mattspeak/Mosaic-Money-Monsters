# ✅ R17 OT 15-17 \- Lukáš, OT, 16

## **Interview 17 Summary**

Segment: Older Teen (15–17)
Male, Lukáš, 16 years old

Participant is a high school student (1st year) who enjoys sports and occasionally works part-time jobs. He shows curiosity about financial topics, particularly investing, but his knowledge remains fragmented and mostly self-directed through online sources.

**Relationship with money**

* Currently attends high school and occasionally earns money through **temporary jobs and seasonal work**.
* Examples of income sources include:
  * summer work with his father
  * helping his grandmother or neighbors
  * occasional short-term jobs.

* He has also experienced situations where **money was deducted from subscriptions he forgot about**, which made him more aware of monitoring expenses.
* In an ideal financial app he would like:
* a **clear balance overview**
* tracking of **expenses**
* **educational tutorials or videos about investing**
* notes explaining **what financial actions lead to certain outcomes**.

**Self-assessment of financial knowledge**

* However, he acknowledged that there are **many financial concepts he does not yet understand**.

**Sources of financial information**

* His main sources of financial knowledge include:
  * parents
  * internet research
  * Google
  * AI tools such as **ChatGPT and Gemini**.
* He sometimes searches online for information about **cryptocurrencies and investments**.
* He does not usually discuss finances with friends, except occasionally mentioning **part-time jobs**.
* He mentioned interest in investing in the future, possibly in **construction or real estate**, although he has not started investing yet.

**School financial education**

* He had financial literacy lessons at school, but described them as **boring and not very engaging**.

**Proposition preference**

* Preferred proposition: **"Money Stuff Nobody Explains."**
* He found this framing **more interesting and relatable**.
* The proposition **"Train Your Money Brain"** sounded to him **too complicated and similar to school language**, which made it less appealing.

**App experience**

**First impression**

* He immediately compared the app to **Duolingo**, noticing the level structure and learning progression.
* He expected that lessons would start **simple and become more difficult over time**.
* He liked the **monster characters**, describing them as more interesting than a formal banker-type character.
* He also found **Mr. Korunka acceptable**, but preferred the monsters because they made the experience more engaging.
* He found the onboarding questions appropriate and not too difficult.

**Learning experience**

* He mentioned learning several new things during the lesson.
* For example, he had **never encountered the 50–30–20 budgeting rule before**.
* However, he was unsure how the percentages should be distributed, suggesting the explanation could be clearer.

**Content fit and expectations**

* He would prefer:
  * **more explanations and educational material**
  * clearer definitions of financial terms.
* Examples of terms he did not understand include:
* **interest**
* **term deposits**
* certain budgeting categories.

* He suggested adding **more detailed tooltips or explanations** for these concepts.

**Gamification and engagement**

* He liked the idea of **collecting monster characters**, which he found motivating.
* He suggested additional engagement features such as:
  * **animations (e.g., confetti) when completing tasks**
  * a **distinctive sound when unlocking rewards**
  * seeing **statistics about how many users completed a lesson or unlocked a character**
  * comparing his progress with other users.

* He also mentioned that parts of the app sometimes felt **empty**, suggesting more visual elements or decorations could improve the experience.

**Standalone vs embedded**

* He would prefer the app to be **a standalone application**.

**Monetisation expectations**

* He believes monetisation would be **demotivating**, and he would prefer the app to remain free.

**Overall rating**

* Rated the app **7/10**.
* He liked the concept and learning potential but felt the app would benefit from:
  * **more explanations**
  * **richer visuals**
    **additional interactive elements**.

**Overall segment signal (individual level)**

* he is curious about financial topics such as investing
* has some experience with **self-directed online learning**
* lacks understanding of fundamental financial concepts
* responds positively to **gamification and collectible mechanics**
* expects **clear explanations of financial terminology**
* values **interactive and visually engaging learning environments**
* prefers financial education tools to remain **separate from banking applications**.
