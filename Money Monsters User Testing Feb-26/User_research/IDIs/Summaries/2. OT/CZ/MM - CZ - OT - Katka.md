# ✅ R09 OT 15-17 \- Katka, OT, 15

## **Interview 9 Summary**

Segment: Older Teen (15-17)
Female, 15 years old, primary school student

Participant is financially engaged mainly in day-to-day spending and shows some awareness of financial topics through school activities. She is interested in tracking spending and learning practical money management skills but has limited experience with more complex financial concepts.

**Relationship with money**

* When asked to design a financial app, she focused primarily on tracking spending and having an overview of what she spends money on.
* She spends most of her money on food and clothes
* Recently spent a larger amount on clothes during a shopping trip with friends.
* Currently saving money to buy a gift for her grandmother.

**Sources of financial information**

* When she wants to learn something about finances, she typically:
  * Googles information
  * Uses AI tools (Copilot)
  * asks her grandfather
* She does not usually discuss finances with friends.

**Emotional baseline**

* Shows moderate confidence in financial topics due to exposure at school.
* Participated in a financial literacy competition between schools and placed second.
* However, still feels there are important aspects she does not fully understand, such as:
  * What exactly happens with money when paying by card in a shop
  * Taxes

**Proposition preference (ranked)**

* "Train Your Money Brain" – strongest resonance.
* Why this proposition resonated:
  * Interpreted it as an opportunity to learn how to manage money better.
  * Associated it with improving decision-making related to finances.

**App experience**

**First impression**

* Perceived design as nice but slightly too pale ("bledý") and lacking stronger colors.
* Suggested colors could be more vivid ("výraznější").
* Would expect more visual dynamics or simulation elements (e.g., simulated purchasing situations).
* Intuitively clicked the green button to start the lesson.
* Liked Mr. Korunka and described him as cute – "roztomilý".
* Onboarding felt easy and straightforward.
* Difficulty of onboarding questions felt appropriate.
* Identified herself as a "controller" type in the onboarding quiz.

**Learning experience**

* Liked the chat format and the characters in it.
* Appreciated explanations within lessons.
* Felt she could learn new things from the content.
* Noticed that lessons felt more difficult than the onboarding quiz.
* Found the progression engaging and educational.
* Some questions were unclear, especially the one related to identifying primary needs.

**Gamification and design response**

* Monsters perceived as cute and engaging.
* Streak feature would motivate her to return regularly.
* Suggested social features such as:
  * Competing with friends
  * Comparing points or progress
  * Leaderboards within the app
* Overview of topics/monsters: suggested adjusting monster overview screen so that monsters are displayed in the same size and aligned next to each other for clearer navigation.

**Content fit and expectations**

* Recognizes that lessons become gradually more difficult.
* Feels she can still learn new information even if she already has some basic financial literacy knowledge.

**Standalone vs embedded**

* Would prefer the app to be integrated into another platform, such as a banking app.
* If it were a standalone app, she believes it would require strong promotion or advertising for people to discover it.

**Monetisation expectations**

* Open to paid content if the price is reasonable.
* However, personally not very likely to pay.
* Would prefer paying for additional content rather than for in-app coins. Would not like paying for extra coins to unlock content.
* Mentioned that with games she prefers buying the whole game once rather than unlocking features gradually through microtransactions.

**Overall rating**

* Rated the app 8–9 out of 10\.
* The slightly lower score was mainly due to design aspects (colors, clarity of some questions).

**Overall segment signal (individual level)**

* This participant shows interest in financial topics and exposure through school activities.
* She values practical explanations and interactive learning formats.
* Responds positively to character-based design and chat-style lessons.
* Would appreciate a stronger visual design (more colours) and clearer wording in some questions.
* Social competition features could increase engagement.
* Integration into existing digital ecosystems (such as banking apps) could improve adoption.
