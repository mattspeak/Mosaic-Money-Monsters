# ✅ R16 OT 15-17 \- Vilma, OT, 15

## **Interview 16 Summary**

Segment: Older Teen (15–17)
Female, Vilma, 15 years old

Participant lives in Prague and plays basketball in her free time. She already uses basic banking tools and shows interest in managing her money, although most of her financial decisions remain simple and focused on everyday spending.

**Relationship with money**

* Has a bank account at mBank and has direct access to the banking app where she can monitor her balance and transactions.
* When she wants to deposit cash into her account, she gives the money to her mother, who transfers it to the account.
* Her income comes mainly from:
  * parents
  * grandparents
  * occasional rewards (e.g., receiving money for good grades).
* In an ideal financial app she would like:
  * an overview of how much money she has on the account
  * access to card information
  * the possibility to generate QR codes for payments
  * notifications about transactions or financial activity.
* She mentioned that some financial topics are not relevant to her yet, such as managing money when living independently.

**Self-assessment of financial knowledge**

7 – she feels she can judge / recognize what is appropriate, she usually doesn't spend money unnecessarily, but remembers that few times she regretted what she bought

* She sees financial decisions mainly as everyday micro-decisions, such as:
* whether to buy something or not
* whether to pay by cash or card
* whether to deposit money into her account or keep it in cash.


**Proposition preference**

* Preferred proposition: "Train Your Money Brain."
* Why this proposition resonated
* She described it as:
  * short
  * clear
  * motivating.
* For her, financial learning is connected mainly to daily decisions, and the wording encouraged her to think more carefully about spending and money management.

**App experience**

**First impression**

* She described the first screen as resembling a game with levels (later she said it's similar to Duolingo).
* However, she initially felt the design looked too childish, especially because of the monster characters.
* She estimated that the design looked most suitable for children up to around 12 years old.
* She found the onboarding understandable.


**Learning experience**

* She suggested replacing the character name "Tracy" with a more Czech-sounding name.
* Some text expressions felt unnatural to her (for example stylized sounds such as "grr" or "pst"), which she felt did not reflect how people normally communicate.
* She appreciated that lessons were presented through stories and scenarios, which made them easier to follow.
* However, she expected clearer explanations of financial terms, particularly concepts she had never encountered before (for example variable costs).

**Design response**

* She suggested several design improvements:
  * different color than green
  * revealing monster characters only after completing a lesson, rather than showing them at the beginning
  * keeping the element of surprise to make progression more exciting.
* She liked the streak feature, which she compared to Duolingo.
* Her perception of the design evolved during the session:
  * initially she did not like the design
  * after progressing through the lessons and unlocking characters she found the experience more enjoyable and less childish.

**Content fit and expectations**

* She felt that some topics, such as loans, are currently irrelevant to her life because she does not deal with borrowing yet.
* She believes these topics might become useful later in adulthood.


**Standalone vs embedded**

* She would prefer the app to be a standalone application rather than part of a banking app.
* She explained that:
  * she would like the freedom to open the app whenever she wants
  * if she did not want to use it, she would not want it integrated into another app she uses regularly.

**Monetisation expectations**

* She would not want paid features.
* She mentioned that she generally does not spend money on apps or in-app purchases, so paid features would feel unnecessary to her.

**Overall rating**

* Rated the app 7/10.
* The main reason for the lower score was the visual design, which initially felt too childish.
* She also mentioned that the app would need to be very engaging and entertaining for her to use it regularly.

**Additional suggestions**

* She would like the app to include social features, such as:
  * comparing progress with friends
  * connecting with friends in the app
  * possibly maintaining a shared streak.

**Overall segment signal (individual level)**

* she already uses banking tools and manages small amounts of money independently
* views financial decisions mainly as everyday spending choices
* responds positively to gamified learning structures
* is sensitive to design maturity and initially perceived the visual style as too childish
* values clear explanations of unfamiliar financial terms
* is motivated by social interaction and comparison with friends
* prefers educational tools that remain separate from banking applications.
