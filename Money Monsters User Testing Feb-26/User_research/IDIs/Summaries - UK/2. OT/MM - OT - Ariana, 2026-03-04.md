### User Research Interview - Financial Education App

- 17-year-old participant testing prototype for financial education app
- Currently uses Santander, PayPal, and Monzo for money management
- Earns money through paper rounds, receives cash from parents electronically
- Self-rates financial knowledge as 5/10 compared to peers

### Money Management Approach

- Primary focus on savings allocation and weekly spending limits
- Uses designated savings buckets for specific purposes
	- Currently saving for new school bag
	- Allocates weekly amounts for food, spontaneous fun activities
- Prefers seeing total savings balance prominently displayed
- Transfers money to Monzo for larger savings goals to keep separate from current account
- Doesn’t use advanced banking features like pots or roundups

### Financial Knowledge Gaps & Learning Sources

- Major knowledge gap: tax payment processes and obligations
	- Turning 18 soon, concerned about tax responsibilities
	- Doesn’t know when/how taxes will be required
- Learning sources ranked by importance:
- Previously researched pensions after overhearing conversation
- Finds money “overwhelming” - everything spirals into complex interconnected topics

### App Prototype Feedback

- Initial reaction: reminds of Duolingo homepage (familiar, positive)
- Monster collection feature felt childish and meaningless
	- Suggested customization rewards to make monsters more engaging
- Quiz questions felt too personal rather than educational
	- Preferred knowledge testing over personality assessment
- Lesson quality varied significantly:
	- First lesson (budgeting): answers felt obscure, didn’t learn much
	- Second lesson (interest/savings): more informative and clear
- Visual simulations enhanced learning experience
- Overall rating: 7/10

### App Improvement Recommendations

- Enable topic selection rather than forced linear progression
	- Current content too basic for target age group (15-18)
	- Need more advanced topics relevant to near-adults
- Add tangible incentives for daily use
	- Referenced WeWard app’s point-based reward system
	- Suggested customization options or social features
- Include more government schemes and credit score education
- Wouldn’t pay for premium educational content (would research independently)
- Might pay for ad removal if ads became intrusive
