# Money Monsters Research Scope
**v1 10th February 2026**

---

## What We're Doing

 We're conducting 36 one-to-one video interviews with young people across the UK and Czech Republic to establish whether Money Monsters resonates with its target audience and identify clear launch-readiness priorities. This isn't just usability testing — it's **product-market fit validation**.

---

## Research Objectives

We're testing five key questions:

| Objective | What We Need to Learn |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **Proposition clarity** | Do users instantly understand what Money Monsters is and why it matters to them? |
| **Triggers & barriers** | What would make them download, try, and return — vs. what would make them give up? |
| **Usability & comprehension** | Can they navigate the app and understand the content without help? |
| **Engagement & motivation** | Does the gamification create momentum, or does it feel gimmicky? |
| **Competitive choice** | Why would they choose this over TikTok, Google, ChatGPT, or other options? |

---

## Who We're Talking To

| Segment | Age | Total Interviews | App Focus |
|---------|-----|------------------|-----------|
| Young Teens | 12–14 | 12 (6 UK, 6 CZ) | Levels 1–2 |
| Older Teens | 15–17 | 12 (6 UK, 6 CZ) | Levels 3–5 |
| Young Adults | 18–25 | 12 (6 UK, 6 CZ) | Levels 5–8 |

**Key recruitment principles:**
- Mainstream young people (not finance enthusiasts)
- Priority given to lower financial literacy and lower-income households
- Excluding: financially comfortable households, anyone with formal financial training, households where parents work in finance/banking

---

## How We'll Spend the Time

Each session is 60 minutes on Zoom (video + audio recorded). Here's how we'll structure it:

| Section                | Time    | What We're Finding Out                                                                               |
| ---------------------- | ------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Warm-up**            | 10 min  | Building rapport so they feel comfortable giving honest feedback                                     |
| **Money context**      | 8 min   | How money fits into their life, where they currently go for guidance                                 |
| **Expectations**       | 5 min   | What they'd want from a money learning tool before we show them anything                             |
| **First impressions**  | 8 min   | Does the proposition land instantly? Do they see it as "for them"?                                   |
| **App walkthrough**    | 15 min  | Observing real behaviour — where they get stuck, what they understand (see detailed breakdown below) |
| **Reflection & close** | 10 min  | Honest verdict, likelihood of returning, what's missing                                              |
| *Buffer*               | *4 min* | *For overruns or technical issues*                                                                   |

### App Walkthrough: Detailed Breakdown (18 min)

This is the heart of the session — where we observe real behaviour and understand whether the product actually works. 

We deliberately hold off showing the app until this point to avoid priming participants/distracting them.

**Pre-fieldwork requirements:**

| Item | Notes | Responsibility |
|------|-------|----------------|
| Figma link (view-only) | Must not require login. Test on multiple browsers before sessions. | Client to provide & confirm |
| Prototype per segment | May need 3 variants (Young Teens / Older Teens / Young Adults) or single prototype with branching | Client + Moderator to agree |

| Backup: PDF/image export | In case Figma fails mid-session — key screens as static fallback | Moderator to prepare |
| Screen recording consent | Consent form must cover recording of prototype interaction | Part of consent form |

**What we're watching for:**

| Phase | Positive indicators | Concern indicators |
|-------|--------------------|--------------------|
| First impressions | "Oh this looks fun", leaning in, immediate recognition of purpose | Confusion, "what is this?", "is this for kids?", sceptical expression |
| Onboarding quiz | Smiles, nods, engaged posture, "oh I know this", thoughtful pauses | Sighs, rushing through, "this is awkward", defensive body language, eye rolls |
| Home/topic navigation | Clear tap decision, "I'd start here because...", scanning with interest | Long hesitation, "I don't know what these mean", random tapping, overwhelmed |
| Lesson content | Leaning in, re-reading to understand, "oh that makes sense", nodding | Skimming, confused expression, "I don't get it", checking phone, zoning out |
| Gamification | Checking progress, "I want to get more of these", mentioning streaks | Dismissing rewards, "so what?", clicking past without looking, no mention |
