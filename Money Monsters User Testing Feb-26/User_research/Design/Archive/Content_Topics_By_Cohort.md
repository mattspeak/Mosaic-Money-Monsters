# Money Monsters: User-Centred Topic Screens

## The Core Principle

**Lead with the user's relationship with money, not the topic.**

Wrong: "Earn — How money comes to you"
Right: "Where does your money actually come from?"

Wrong: "Protect — Keeping your money safe"
Right: "That deal looks too good... is it?"

The six areas (Earn, Spend, Save, Borrow, Protect, Grow) are internal categories. Users see questions about THEIR lives.

---

## How This Works

Each topic has:
1. **User-centred hook** — The question/situation they see on the selection screen
2. **Introduction** — What they see when they tap (establishes relevance)
3. **3 Lessons** — Each framed as a user-centred situation, not a concept

# YOUNG TEENS (13-15)

*Based on: Lily, 14, Leicester. £5-6/week pocket money. 90% digital spending. Worries about money stress at home. Wants to feel smart about adult stuff without it feeling like school.*

---

## EARN

### Selection Screen Hook
**"Where does your money actually come from?"**

### Introduction
You probably get money from a few places — pocket money, helping out, birthdays, maybe selling stuff. But have you ever thought about WHY some people have more than others? Or how YOU could end up with more?

This isn't about getting a job (that comes later). It's about understanding the money game before you're playing it for real.

### Lessons

**Lesson 1: "Why does my friend get more pocket money than me?"**
Not all pocket money is equal — and it's not always about fairness. Learn what actually decides how much people get, and what you can do about yours.

**Lesson 2: "Could I actually make money from something I'm good at?"**
Some teenagers earn money before they're old enough for a "proper job." What are people your age actually doing? (It's not all mowing lawns.)

**Lesson 3: "Why do some jobs pay more than others?"**
A YouTuber can earn millions. A nurse saves lives but earns way less. What's going on? Understanding this now changes how you think about your future.

---

## SPEND

### Selection Screen Hook
**"Why does my money disappear so fast?"**

### Introduction
You had £20. Now you have £3. Where did it go? You're not bad with money — you're just playing against apps, shops, and games that are DESIGNED to make you spend without noticing.

Once you see the tricks, you can't unsee them.

### Lessons

**Lesson 1: "Why do I always want to buy stuff in games?"**
That "limited time offer" in your game isn't random. Learn the psychology behind why games make you WANT to spend — and how to spot when you're being played.

**Lesson 2: "Is it better to buy the cheap one or save for the good one?"**
Sometimes cheap is smart. Sometimes cheap costs you more. Learn the questions to ask before you buy ANYTHING.

**Lesson 3: "My friend bought something stupid — why didn't I stop myself?"**
You've probably bought something you regretted (85% of teenagers have). Why do we do this? And what's the 10-second trick that actually works?

---

## SAVE

### Selection Screen Hook
**"Is saving actually worth it when I don't have much?"**

### Introduction
"Just save your money" is advice adults love giving. But when you've only got a few pounds, it feels pointless. Why save £2 when you could just... have something now?

Here's the thing they don't tell you: small money has a secret superpower. And it works better the younger you are.

### Lessons

**Lesson 1: "What's the point of saving £5?"**
It feels like nothing. But £5 has a trick to it — if you know where to put it and how long to leave it. This is actually the most important money lesson you'll ever learn.

**Lesson 2: "How do I save when there's always something I want?"**
The "willpower" approach doesn't work. Your brain is wired to want stuff NOW. Learn the sneaky methods that work even when you don't feel like saving.

**Lesson 3: "Where should I actually put my money?"**
Piggy bank? Bank account? Under your mattress? Each option is different — and some actually make your money grow while it sits there.

---

## BORROW

### Selection Screen Hook
**"What's the catch with 'buy now, pay later'?"**

### Introduction
Klarna. Clearpay. "Pay in 3." It sounds amazing — get the thing NOW, worry about money LATER. But companies aren't offering this because they're nice.

Before you click "pay later," you should know what you're actually signing up for.

### Lessons

**Lesson 1: "Why would anyone pay MORE to get something now?"**
Borrowing means paying back extra. Why would anyone do that? Actually, there are times it makes sense — and times it's a trap. Learn the difference.

**Lesson 2: "My mate says Klarna is fine — is it?"**
Buy now, pay later apps seem harmless. But one in four people who use them miss payments. What actually happens when you can't pay? (It's worse than you think.)

**Lesson 3: "How do people end up in debt they can't escape?"**
You've probably heard of people drowning in debt. How does that even happen? It usually starts with something small that seemed totally reasonable at the time.

---

## PROTECT

### Selection Screen Hook
**"That deal looks too good... should I trust it?"**

### Introduction
Free V-Bucks. Giveaways. "DM me for a guaranteed way to make money." The internet is FULL of offers that sound amazing. Most of them are designed to take your money or your data.

Getting scammed doesn't mean you're stupid. Scammers are professionals. But once you know their playbook, they can't fool you.

### Lessons

**Lesson 1: "How do I know if a giveaway is real or a scam?"**
That Instagram giveaway your favourite creator shared... is it legit? Learn the 5 red flags that scammers can't hide.

**Lesson 2: "Someone online says they can double my money — what's really going on?"**
Crypto doubling. Forex signals. "Investment opportunities." These scams catch thousands of teenagers. Here's how they work and why smart people still fall for them.

**Lesson 3: "What's the worst that can happen if I give out my details?"**
It's just your email... or your postcode... or your date of birth. What can someone actually DO with that information? (More than you think.)

---

## GROW

### Selection Screen Hook
**"Can money actually make more money?"**

### Introduction
Rich people don't just earn more — their money MAKES more money while they sleep. That sounds unfair. But here's the thing: you can do it too. And starting young is basically a cheat code.

This is the stuff most adults wish they'd understood earlier.

### Lessons

**Lesson 1: "What does 'interest' actually mean?"**
You've heard the word. But what IS interest? Why do banks pay YOU to leave money with them? And why does it matter so much if you're young?

**Lesson 2: "What's the difference between saving and investing?"**
They sound similar but they're completely different games. One is safe and slow. One is riskier but can grow way faster. Which one is right for what?

**Lesson 3: "Could I actually invest money at my age?"**
Some teenagers are already doing it (with their parents' help). What are they buying? How does it work? And should you even bother?

# OLDER TEENS (16-18)

*Based on: Jake, 17, Manchester. Earns £300-400/month part-time. Saving for driving lessons. University decision looming with £45k debt. Skeptical of finfluencers. Wants practical help, not lectures.*

---

## EARN

### Selection Screen Hook
**"Why is so much missing from my payslip?"**

### Introduction
You worked the hours. You did the maths. But the money in your account is... less? Welcome to tax, National Insurance, and all the deductions nobody explains.

Understanding your payslip isn't exciting. But it's the difference between knowing if you're being paid correctly — and just trusting that you are.

### Lessons

**Lesson 1: "What are all these deductions on my payslip?"**
Tax, NI, pension... your payslip has numbers everywhere. What do they actually mean? And are they taking the right amount? (Spoiler: sometimes they're not.)

**Lesson 2: "Am I being paid fairly compared to other people my age?"**
Minimum wage has different rates for different ages. Some employers try to underpay. Know what you're entitled to and how to check.

**Lesson 3: "Side hustle or regular job — what's actually better?"**
Depop reselling, tutoring, freelance work... it feels more flexible than a job. But what about tax? What about employment rights? The honest trade-offs.

---

## SPEND

### Selection Screen Hook
**"How do I make £350 last the whole month?"**

### Introduction
When you're earning your own money, spending decisions get real. £350 sounds like a lot until you need to make it last four weeks while your mates want to go out every weekend.

This isn't about never spending. It's about spending on PURPOSE so you don't hit £0 with a week still to go.

### Lessons

**Lesson 1: "Why am I always broke before payday?"**
If your money runs out before the month does, you're not alone — 52% of students have the same problem. Here's how to spot where it's actually going.

**Lesson 2: "How do I budget without it taking over my life?"**
Spreadsheets are boring. Tracking every penny is exhausting. But there ARE methods that work without making you miserable. Find one that fits how you actually live.

**Lesson 3: "My mates want to go out but I can't afford it — what do I do?"**
Money affects friendships. Saying "I'm broke" feels embarrassing. How do you manage socially when you're trying to be smart with money? Real strategies that work.

---

## SAVE

### Selection Screen Hook
**"How do I save when there's always something I need?"**

### Introduction
Driving lessons: £1,500+. Car insurance: £1,200+. University costs. Moving out. The numbers are terrifying — and "just save more" doesn't help when you're already stretched.

But here's what nobody tells you: even small, messy saving beats perfect saving that never happens.

### Lessons

**Lesson 1: "What's the minimum I need saved before I feel safe?"**
An "emergency fund" sounds adult and boring. But imagine your phone dies tomorrow. Or you need to get home urgently. How much do you actually need as a buffer?

**Lesson 2: "Automatic saving sounds good — but how do I set it up?"**
The best saving happens without thinking about it. But how do you actually make it automatic? Step-by-step, from whatever banking app you use.

**Lesson 3: "Savings accounts all look the same — do they actually matter?"**
0.5% vs 4.5% sounds like nothing. But over time? It's hundreds of pounds difference. What to look for, what to avoid, and whether ISAs are worth it yet.

---

## BORROW

### Selection Screen Hook
**"Student loans, overdrafts, credit scores — which actually matter?"**

### Introduction
At 18, you'll face decisions about debt that follow you for decades. Student loans that seem abstract. Overdrafts that feel like free money. Credit scores you didn't know you had.

Nobody expects you to know this already. But making these decisions WITHOUT understanding them is how people get burned.

### Lessons

**Lesson 1: "How do student loans actually work? (Honest version)"**
You've heard the numbers (£45k+ debt) and the reassurances ("you only pay when you earn"). What's the actual truth? The mechanics of how repayment really works.

**Lesson 2: "My bank offered me an overdraft — should I use it?"**
Free money when you need it! Except... it's not free. And it's not quite that simple. What overdrafts actually cost and when they make sense (vs when they don't).

**Lesson 3: "What even is a credit score and why should I care now?"**
You might not want to borrow money for years. But your credit score is being built RIGHT NOW. What affects it, what doesn't, and why checking it is free and important.

---

## PROTECT

### Selection Screen Hook
**"How do I know if financial advice online is legit?"**

### Introduction
TikTok finance tips. Discord trading groups. "I made £5k in a week" posts. Some of it's helpful. Some of it's illegal. Some of it's designed to take your money.

You're smart enough to know not everything online is true. But financial scams are designed to fool smart people.

### Lessons

**Lesson 1: "That finfluencer sounds confident — can I trust them?"**
2 in 5 young people think TikTok influencers give better advice than banks. But half of investment scams happen on social media. How to tell the difference.

**Lesson 2: "Crypto, forex, trading — is any of it actually legit for beginners?"**
You've seen the screenshots of massive gains. You've heard it's the future. What's real, what's risky, and what's an outright scam? No hype, no fear — just facts.

**Lesson 3: "What rights do I actually have as a young worker?"**
Dodgy employers exist. Being paid late, being asked to work off the clock, being pressured to skip breaks. Know what's legal, what's not, and who can help.

---

## GROW

### Selection Screen Hook
**"Should I care about investing when I barely have savings?"**

### Introduction
Investing feels like something for people with money. You've got £50 to your name — why would you invest?

But here's the thing: understanding HOW money grows matters MORE when you don't have much. Because the earlier you start, the less you actually need to put in.

### Lessons

**Lesson 1: "What's compound interest and why does everyone keep mentioning it?"**
"The eighth wonder of the world." "The most powerful force in the universe." Why do people get so excited about this? A simple explanation with actual numbers.

**Lesson 2: "Stocks, shares, funds, crypto — what's the actual difference?"**
The words get thrown around like everyone knows them. But what ARE these things? A no-jargon guide to what you're actually buying when you "invest."

**Lesson 3: "Could I realistically start investing as a student?"**
Some people your age are doing it. Apps make it possible with tiny amounts. Is it worth it? What are the risks? An honest look at whether it makes sense for you.

# YOUNG ADULTS (18-25)

*Based on: Ryan, 21, Nottingham. Non-graduate earning £23k (~£1,550/month after tax). 47% on rent. Has workplace pension but ignores it. "Live for today" mindset. Wants practical help, not lectures about saving more.*

---

## EARN

### Selection Screen Hook
**"Am I being paid what I'm worth?"**

### Introduction
You're working full-time. You're paying taxes. You've got more responsibilities than you had at 16 — but has your financial knowledge kept up?

Payslips, tax codes, pension contributions... this stuff isn't complicated, but nobody teaches it. And not knowing costs you money.

### Lessons

**Lesson 1: "My tax code is wrong — how would I even know?"**
It happens more than you think. And it can mean hundreds of pounds over- or under-paid. How to read your tax code and what to do if it doesn't look right.

**Lesson 2: "Freelancing sounds appealing — what's the financial reality?"**
Side hustles, self-employment, gig work. The flexibility is real. But so is the admin. Tax, expenses, no sick pay — what nobody mentions before you start.

**Lesson 3: "How do I ask for a pay rise without it being awkward?"**
You've been in the job a while. You're doing more than you were hired for. How do you actually have that conversation? What works, what doesn't.

---

## SPEND

### Selection Screen Hook
**"Rent, bills, food — is there meant to be anything left?"**

### Introduction
When 47% of your income goes on rent before you've bought food, budgeting advice like "cut the avocado toast" feels insulting.

This isn't about judging your spending. It's about finding where the ACTUAL flexibility is — and making what little is left work harder.

### Lessons

**Lesson 1: "My money's gone and I don't know where — how do I track it without obsessing?"**
Budgeting apps can make you MORE anxious. But having no idea where money goes is worse. Methods that work for people who hate tracking.

**Lesson 2: "What should I actually be spending on rent?"**
47% of income on rent is the average for young people. But is that sustainable? What happens if it's higher? Honest answers, not judgment.

**Lesson 3: "Should I feel guilty about spending on myself?"**
You work hard. You have almost nothing left. But sometimes you want a takeaway or a night out. How to handle spending on "wants" when money is tight — without spiraling.

## SAVE

### Selection Screen Hook
**"Is saving even possible when I'm barely surviving?"**

### Introduction
"Pay yourself first." "Build an emergency fund." The advice assumes you have money left over. What if you don't?

This is about realistic saving for people who aren't earning much. It won't make you rich. But it might keep you from being wiped out by one bad month.

### Lessons

**Lesson 1: "Emergency fund — is £500 actually enough?"**
The internet says 3-6 months' expenses. That's tens of thousands. What about £500? What can that actually protect you from?

**Lesson 2: "I've tried saving and it never works — what am I doing wrong?"**
You set money aside. Something comes up. It's gone. The problem isn't willpower — it's strategy. What actually works when you've failed before.

**Lesson 3: "LISAs, ISAs, regular savings — which ones actually matter?"**
These names get thrown around. But what ARE they? Which one is right when you can only afford to put aside £25/month? Plain English answers.

## BORROW

### Selection Screen Hook
**"Is my debt normal or am I in trouble?"**

### Introduction
Credit card. Overdraft. Maybe buy now pay later. Maybe more. At some point, "manageable" debt starts to feel like a weight.

This isn't about making you feel bad. It's about understanding when debt is fine, when it's a problem, and what to actually do about it.

### Lessons

**Lesson 1: "Minimum payments feel manageable — so why do people warn against them?"**
£25/month on a credit card seems fine. Until you see how long it takes to pay off... and how much extra you pay. The maths might change how you think about it.

**Lesson 2: "My credit score is bad — can I actually fix it?"**
Maybe you missed payments. Maybe you have no history. Either way, a bad credit score follows you. What actually affects it and what you can do to rebuild.

**Lesson 3: "I'm behind on payments — what are my actual options?"**
It's embarrassing. It's stressful. But ignoring it makes it worse. What to do, who to contact, and what help actually exists (more than you think).

## PROTECT

### Selection Screen Hook
**"Am I being scammed or am I being paranoid?"**

### Introduction
Rental deposits that vanish. Job offers that want money upfront. "Investment opportunities" that guarantee returns. When you're young and stretched, you're a target.

The worst part: scams often look completely legitimate. Knowing the patterns is your best protection.

### Lessons

**Lesson 1: "That flat looks perfect and cheap — how do I know if it's real?"**
Rental scams cost young people thousands. They use real listings, real-sounding landlords, and urgent pressure. The specific red flags to look for before you pay anything.

**Lesson 2: "What insurance do I actually need?"**
Contents insurance. Phone insurance. Life insurance. Pet insurance. It feels like everyone wants your money for "what ifs." What's actually worth it when money is tight?

**Lesson 3: "If I get scammed, is the money just... gone?"**
Banks sometimes refund scam victims. Sometimes they don't. What determines it, and what to do in the first 24 hours to maximize your chances of getting money back.

## GROW

### Selection Screen Hook
**"Pensions, investing — is that for people like me?"**

### Introduction
Pension? You can barely afford rent. Investing? That's for people with actual money. The whole "grow your wealth" conversation feels like it's for someone else.

But here's the uncomfortable truth: waiting until you "earn more" costs you more than you think. Even tiny amounts matter — if you understand why.

### Lessons

**Lesson 1: "My workplace pension takes money from my paycheck — should I opt out?"**
Free money from your employer. But it also means less take-home pay right now. Is it worth it when you're barely getting by? The honest maths.

**Lesson 2: "I have £50 spare once in a while — is investing it actually worth it?"**
The internet shows people making thousands. You have £50. Can that actually grow into anything meaningful? Realistic expectations vs. the hype.

**Lesson 3: "The future feels so far away — why should I care now?"**
49% of Gen Z prefer living for today over planning for tomorrow. That's reasonable when the future is uncertain. But there's a way to balance both without sacrificing everything.

# Testing Recommendations

## Primary Test Question
After showing introduction screens:
> "Does this feel like it's talking about YOUR life, or someone else's?"

## What to Watch For
- Do they recognise the situations in the lesson hooks?
- Do they feel the tone is right (not too childish, not too adult)?
- Does the content make them feel capable or overwhelmed?
- Would they actually tap to learn more?

## Red Flags
- "This is like school"
- "I already know this" (when they probably don't)
- "This isn't for someone like me"
- "I don't get what this has to do with my life"

# Czech Localisation Notes

Beyond translation:
- **Currency:** All £ amounts need Kč equivalents (£5 ≈ 150 Kč, £350 ≈ 10,500 Kč)
- **Products:** ISAs, LISAs, student loans work differently — need Czech equivalents
- **Platforms:** Klarna, Clearpay — check which BNPL is popular in Czech Republic
- **Cultural references:** "V-Bucks" and gaming references should work, but verify
- **Rental market:** Different norms around deposits, contracts, scams
- **Employment:** Different minimum wage ages, worker rights

Recommend full localisation review, not just translation.
