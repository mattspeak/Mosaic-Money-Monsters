# Money Monsters — Lesson 1: How Do You Use Money?

> **Purpose of this document:** This is a structured reference of the app lesson flow extracted from the Figma prototype. Use it to understand the sequence, tone, question logic, and feedback patterns when tailoring or refining long-form lesson content.

---

## Lesson Metadata

- **Lesson Title:** How Do You Use Money?
- **Level:** Level 1
- **Character:** Tom (works a Saturday job)
- **Monster Companion:** Runner (unlocked at end of level)
- **Core Concepts:** Earning, spending, saving, current vs savings accounts, interest
- **Tone:** Conversational, slightly cheeky, relatable to teens

---

## Flow Structure

### 1. Homepage / Entry Point

- **Screen:** Homepage Navigation
- Player sees their avatar/monster ("Money Trailblazer")
- Entry prompt: **"How do you use money?"**
- CTA: Tap to begin lesson

---

### 2. Lesson Intro Sequence

#### Screen 2a — Lesson Intro 1
- **Visual:** Illustrated characters sitting around a table
- **Headline:** "HOW DO YOU USE MONEY?"
- **Body:** Introductory framing — this lesson helps you get your first handle on money

#### Screen 2b — Lesson Intro 2
- **Headline:** "What do you know?"
- **Body:** Reflective prompt asking what the player already knows about using money
- Sets up a self-assessment tone before teaching begins

#### Screen 2c — Lesson Intro 2 (continued)
- **Headline:** "Welcome to your financial journey"
- **Body:** Introduces the concept of a financial journey and the Money Monsters world
- **Reflective question:** "What if your family couldn't...?" (empathy hook)
- **CTA:** "Start Mini-lesson" → Next

---

### 3. Concept Introduction — Meet Tom

#### Screen 3a — Concept 1
- **Visual:** Tom character (illustrated young person)
- **Body:** "This is Tom. He works Saturdays at..." (part-time job context)
- Establishes Tom as a relatable character who earns money
- Sets up the scenario: Tom earns a specific amount and needs to decide what to do with it

#### Screen 3b — Level Intro
- **Visual:** Tom with additional context
- **Body:** Further details on Tom's financial situation — what he earns, what he spends
- Introduces the idea that Tom has money left over after expenses

#### Screen 3c — Level Intro
- **Visual:** Tom scenario continued
- **Body:** Reinforces the setup — Tom's income minus his spending leaves a surplus
- Transition into the first interactive question

---

### 4. Pop Quiz 1 — Calculation

#### Screen 4a — Pop Quiz 1
- **Question:** "How much can Tom get left over?"
- **Format:** Numeric input (player types/selects an amount)
- **Correct Answer:** £20

#### Feedback — Incorrect
- **Screen:** Level Info Overlay
- **Message:** "Nope! Try again!"
- **CTA:** Continue (loops back to question)

#### Feedback — Correct
- **Screen:** Level Info Overlay
- **Message:** Confirmation of correct answer
- **CTA:** Continue to next section

---

### 5. Key Decision — Where to Put Savings

#### Screen 5a — Level Intro (Question Setup)
- **Visual:** Piggy bank illustration
- **Question:** "Where should Tom put his spare £20?"
- **Format:** Multiple choice
- **Options (inferred from feedback screens):**
  - A) Current account
  - B) Savings account ✅
  - C) Under the mattress / somewhere unsafe
  - D) Spend it

#### Feedback — Option A: Current Account
- **Screen:** Level Info Overlay
- **Message:** "A current account is great for spending — but it pays little or no interest. Tom wants his money to grow, not just sit there."
- **CTA:** Forward / Try again

#### Feedback — Option B: Savings Account ✅ (Correct)
- **Screen:** Level Info Overlay
- **Message:** "A savings account pays you interest. A current account is for your day-to-day spending."
- **CTA:** Continue

#### Feedback — Option C: Unsafe option
- **Screen:** Level Info Overlay
- **Message:** "Are you sure about that? It's not that safe AND it's not earning anything! The right account makes money!"
- **CTA:** Try again

#### Feedback — Option D: Doesn't matter
- **Screen:** Level Info Overlay
- **Message:** "It really does matter — a savings account earns interest, a current account barely does."
- **CTA:** Try again

---

### 6. Concept Exploration — Interest

#### Screen 6a — Level Intro
- **Visual:** Characters in discussion
- **Question:** "You've heard of interest... but what do you think it actually is?"
- **Format:** Multiple choice (inferred from feedback screens)
- **Options (inferred):**
  - A) Extra money the bank pays you for saving ✅
  - B) A fee for checking your balance
  - C) Money you earn by spending

#### Feedback — Option A: Extra money for saving ✅ (Correct)
- **Screen:** Level Info Overlay
- **Message:** "Interest is extra money the bank pays you for keeping your money in a savings account. Your money grows over time."
- **CTA:** Continue

#### Feedback — Option B: Checking balance fee
- **Screen:** Level Info Overlay
- **Message:** "Checking your balance is usually free. Interest is actually extra money the bank gives you for saving."
- **CTA:** Try again

#### Feedback — Option C: Spending money
- **Screen:** Level Info Overlay
- **Message:** "Spending money does not earn interest. Interest is extra money you earn by saving money in the bank."
- **CTA:** Try again

---

### 7. Scenario Challenge — Temptation to Spend Savings

#### Screen 7a — Level Intro (Scenario Setup)
- **Visual:** Tom with savings progress tracker
- **Context:** "Tom has been saving £20 a month for 4 months for a £100 pair of headphones. Only 1 month to go!"
- **Visual:** Popup text message interface: New text from Ranveer
- **CTA:** "Reply to Ranveer"

#### Screen 7b — Reply to Ranveer (Scenario Setup)

- **Question:** Reply to Ranveer
- **Format:** Multiple choice
- **Options (inferred from feedback screens):**
  - A) Dip into savings for a night out
  - B) Use current account / spending money for the night out ✅
  - C) Skip the night out entirely and keep saving
  - D) Go out and spend the savings — YOLO

#### Feedback — Option A: Dip into savings
- **Screen:** Level Info Overlay
- **Message:** "Tempting, but this is how savings get chipped away. One dip becomes two, then three..."
- **CTA:** Continue / Try again

#### Feedback — Option B: Use current account ✅ (Correct)
- **Screen:** Level Info Overlay
- **Message:** "Savings = great. Current account = your life. If you can afford it, use your current account to go spending..."
- **CTA:** Continue

#### Feedback — Option C: Skip the night out
- **Screen:** Level Info Overlay
- **Message:** "Serious discipline! But you don't always have to miss out — if you've got spending money, use that"
- **CTA:** Continue (accepted but nudged)

#### Feedback — Option D: Spend savings — YOLO
- **Screen:** Level Info Overlay
- **Message:** "Your call — but 1 month of saving gone in one night? Future you might not love that."
- **CTA:** Try again

## Narrative & Pedagogical Notes

### Tone
- Conversational and slightly cheeky ("Future you might not love that")
- Non-judgmental but firm on correct financial behaviour
- Uses relatable teen scenarios (Saturday jobs, headphones, nights out)

### Feedback Pattern
- **Correct:** Brief positive reinforcement + concept explanation
- **Incorrect:** Explains WHY the answer is wrong in practical terms, then loops back
- **Partially correct (e.g., skipping the night out):** Acknowledged as valid but gently redirected to the optimal answer
- No shaming — incorrect feedback always teaches

### Progression Logic
- Linear flow through intro → concept → question → feedback → next concept
- Questions build on each other: earn → save → where to save → what is interest → protect savings
- Monster companion unlocked at end provides emotional reward + narrative hook for next lesson

### Key Teaching Sequence
1. **Context:** Meet Tom, understand his income
2. **Calculate:** Work out what's left after spending
3. **Decide:** Where to put spare money (savings vs current account)
4. **Learn:** What interest is and why it matters
5. **Apply:** Real-world temptation scenario — protect your savings goal
6. **Reward:** Level complete, monster unlocked, social reinforcement
