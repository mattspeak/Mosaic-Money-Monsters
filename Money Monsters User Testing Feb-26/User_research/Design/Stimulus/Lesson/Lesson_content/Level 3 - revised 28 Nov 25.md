Definition

Text: Exchange rate: The value of one currency in terms of another

Text: Ranveer\'s Example:

**Today**: £1 = €1.15

Ranveer\'s £1,500 = €1,725

But in 3 months, it could be:

£1 = €1.10 (pound weakens) → £1,500 = €1,650

£1 = €1.20 (pound strengthens) → £1,500 = €1,800

Text: Factors That Affect Exchange Rates:

Strong economy → currency strengthens

High interest rates → currency strengthens

Political stability → currency strengthens

High inflation → currency weakens

Political uncertainty → currency weakens

**Text - Ranveer**: So I could end up with more or fewer euros depending
on what happens to the pound?

**Text - Ciaran**: Exactly! Exchange rates are always moving based on
world events.

**Lingo Learned - Level 3**

**Currency**: The official money used in a specific country or region

**Exchange Rate**: The value of one currency in terms of another

**Eurozone**: The 19 European countries that use the Euro as their
currency

**Currency Union**: A group of countries that share the same currency

**Strong Currency**: A currency that can buy more of other currencies
than before

**Weak Currency**: A currency that can buy less of other currencies than
before

**Foreign Transaction Fee**: An extra charge (typically 2-3%) for using
cards abroad

**Prepaid Travel Card**: A card you load with currency before travelling
to lock in rates

**Reserve Currency**: A currency held by governments and institutions
worldwide (the US Dollar is the main one)

**Fluctuate**: When exchange rates go up and down over time

**Import**: Goods brought into a country from abroad

**Export**: Goods sent from a country to be sold abroad

**ATM**: Automated Teller Machine - a machine for withdrawing cash

**Diversify**: To spread across different options to reduce risk
