**Lingo Learned - Level 4**

Budget: A plan for how you\'ll spend and save your money

Income: Money coming into your life (wages, allowance, gifts)

Expenses: Money going out (things you spend on)

Needs: Essential expenses you can\'t live without (housing, food,
transport)

Wants: Non-essential expenses that make life more enjoyable
(entertainment, luxuries)

50/30/20 Rule: A budgeting guideline - 50% needs, 30% wants, 20% savings

Cash Flow: The movement of money in and out of your finances

Positive Cash Flow: When income exceeds expenses

Negative Cash Flow: When expenses exceed income (unsustainable)

Fixed Expenses: Costs that stay the same each period (subscriptions,
rent)

Variable Expenses: Costs that change (food, entertainment)

SMART Goals: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound

Emergency Fund: Money set aside for unexpected necessary expenses

Standing Order: An automatic regular payment you set up

Direct Debit: An automatic payment where the company takes what\'s owed

Pay Yourself First: Saving money immediately when you receive income,
before spending

Budget Review: Regularly checking your budget against actual spending
