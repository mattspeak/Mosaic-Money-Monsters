Comparing accounts: What matters?

**Text - Antonio**: I\'ve found three savings accounts online. How do I

Text: What To Look For:

Annual Equivalent Rate (AER) - shows the true yearly return including
compounding

Interest rate alone is not enough - you need AER for fair comparison

Example: Two accounts, same amount (£7,000):

Account A: 5% AER

Account B: 4.5% AER

**Text - Professor Money:** Account A looks better, but let\'s look
deeper. The difference may seem small, but over time it matters
significantly.

The Rule of 72: How long to double your money

Text - Professor Money: Here\'s a brilliant shortcut that shows how
powerful compound interest really is.

**Text:** The Rule of 72:

Divide 72 by your interest rate to find approximately how many years
until your money doubles.

Formula: Years to double = 72 ÷ Interest rate

Text: Examples:

At 6% interest: 72 ÷ 6 = 12 years to double

At 4% interest: 72 ÷ 4 = 18 years to double

At 8% interest: 72 ÷ 8 = 9 years to double

At 3% interest: 72 ÷ 3 = 24 years to double

**Text - Antonio**: So my £7,000 becomes £14,000 in 12 years at 6%
interest - without adding anything?

**Text** - **Professor Money**: Exactly. That\'s compound interest doing
the work for you over time.

**Text - Vinnie:** Look at the difference between 4% and 6%: 18 years
versus 12 years. That same 2% difference costs you 6 years! This is why
comparing interest rates matters.

Text - **Information box:** The Rule of 72 shows why small differences
in interest rates create big differences in results.

A 1% or 2% higher interest rate can help your money double years
earlier. This is why always comparing AER matters - especially for
long-term savings.

**Lingo Learned - Level 5**

Pay Yourself First: Saving money immediately when you receive income,
before spending on anything else

Multi-Pot Saving: Dividing savings into separate pots or accounts for
different goals

Savings Laddering: Spreading money across accounts with different access
terms and interest rates

Round-Up Saving: Automatically saving spare change by rounding purchases
up to the nearest pound

Interest: Money paid to you for letting a bank use your savings

Simple Interest: Interest calculated only on your original deposit

Compound Interest: Interest calculated on your deposit plus previously
earned interest

AER (Annual Equivalent Rate): Standard rate for comparing interest
across different accounts

Easy-Access Account: Savings account where you can withdraw money
anytime

Notice Account: Account requiring advance notice before withdrawals

Fixed-Term Bond: Account that locks money away for a set period for
higher interest

ISA (Individual Savings Account): Tax-free savings account

Cash ISA: ISA for cash savings, available from age 16

Junior ISA: Tax-free savings for under-18s, managed by parents

Lifetime ISA: ISA with 25% government bonus for first home or retirement

Multi-Factor Authentication: Using multiple verification methods for
security

One-Time Passcode: Temporary security code sent to your phone

FSCS: Protects up to £85,000 per person per banking group if bank fails

Section 75: Credit card protection for purchases £100-£30,000

Direct Debit Guarantee: Protection ensuring refunds for incorrect direct
debit amounts
