Standard measure for comparing credit products

The rate offered to at least 51% of successful applicants

credit products.

**Text - Tip box**: Lower APR means lower cost! Always compare APRs when

**Lingo Learned - Level 6**

Credit: The ability to borrow money with an agreement to repay it later,
usually with interest

Interest: The fee charged for borrowing money, shown as a percentage

APR (Annual Percentage Rate): The yearly cost of borrowing including
interest and fees - lower is better

Representative APR: The rate offered to at least 51% of successful
applicants

Credit Card: A card allowing flexible, ongoing borrowing up to a set
limit

Credit Limit: The maximum amount you can borrow on a credit card or
account

Minimum Payment: The smallest amount you must pay to keep your credit
account in good standing

Interest-Free Period: Time during which no interest is charged if you
pay in full (usually up to 56 days)

Loan: A fixed amount of money borrowed all at once, repaid over a set
period

Mortgage: A large loan specifically for buying property, secured against
the property itself

Student Loan: A loan for education costs with income-based repayments
and eventual write-off

Payday Loan: Short-term, very high-interest loan - best avoided

Credit Score: A number showing how reliably you\'ve managed credit in
the past

Credit Report: A detailed record of your credit history held by credit
reference agencies

Credit Utilisation: The percentage of your available credit that you\'re
using (aim for under 30%)

Electoral Register: Official list of everyone eligible to vote - being
registered helps your credit score

Snowball Method: Debt repayment strategy focusing on smallest debts
first for motivation

Avalanche Method: Debt repayment strategy focusing on highest interest
debts first to save money

Phishing: Fraudulent attempts to steal personal information through fake
emails, texts, or websites

Action Fraud: UK national centre for reporting fraud and cybercrime
