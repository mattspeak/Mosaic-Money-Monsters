**Lingo Learned - Level 7**

Financial Plan: A comprehensive strategy connecting your current
situation to your future goals

Time Horizon: The length of time until you need to achieve a goal
(short, medium, or long-term)

Short-term Goal: A financial target to achieve within 0-2 years

Medium-term Goal: A financial target to achieve within 2-5 years

Long-term Goal: A financial target to achieve in 5+ years

SMART Goals: Goals that are Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant,
and Time-bound

Life Stage Planning: Adjusting financial priorities as your
circumstances change through life

Compound Growth: When your returns earn returns, creating accelerating
growth over time

Workplace Pension: Retirement savings through your employer, often with
employer contributions

Personal Pension: Retirement savings you arrange yourself, giving you
control over investments

State Pension: Basic retirement income provided by the government

Emergency Fund: Savings of 3-6 months expenses for unexpected costs (a
form of protection)

Income Protection Insurance: Insurance that replaces income if you\'re
unable to work

Life Insurance: Insurance that pays out to dependants if you die

Will: Legal document directing where your assets go after death

Power of Attorney: Legal document allowing someone to manage your
finances if you can\'t

Intestacy: When someone dies without a will - the law decides asset
distribution

Lifestyle Creep: When spending increases to match income increases

Financial Dashboard: A way to track all aspects of your financial plan
in one place

Review Schedule: Planned times to check progress and adjust your
financial plan
