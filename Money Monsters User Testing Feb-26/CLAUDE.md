# Money Monsters User Testing Project

## Project Overview

### What is Money Monsters?
A gamified financial literacy app for young people, styled after Duolingo. Users learn to "tame their money monsters" through progressive lessons covering budgeting, saving, interest, and investing fundamentals.

**Key Features:**
- Structured levels with progressive difficulty
- Baseline knowledge check
- Vocabulary collection mechanics
- UK and Czech versions available

**Target Audience:**
- Primary: Young people (13-25)
- Three distinct bands:
  - **Young Teens (13-15):** Forming habits, need strong clarity and scaffolding
  - **Older Teens (16-18):** More real-money relevance, higher scepticism
  - **Young Adults (18-25):** Some to full independence, increasing financial literacy and responsibility

**Recruitment:**

Recruitment Principles

- Mainstream participants: typical young people, not finance enthusiasts
- Skew toward need: prioritise lower financial literacy and lower income
- Exclude the wealthy: remove very financially comfortable households
- No prior formal training: exclude any who’ve completed structured education
- Exclude finance households: no parents working in banking, accounting, FS
## Research Objectives

Establish product-market fit and clear launch-readiness priorities.

### Core Questions to Answer:
1. **Proposition clarity** - Do users instantly get what Money Monsters is and why it matters?
2. **Triggers & barriers** - What prompts users to try/return vs. drop off?
3. **Usability & comprehension** - Can they navigate and understand without help?
4. **Engagement & motivation** - Does gamification create momentum?
5. **Pre-launch priorities** - What to fix now vs. queue for later?
6. **Premium direction** - What could drive retention and justify premium tier?

## Research Approach

### Three-Stage Sprint:

**Stage 1: Design & Preparation (Week 1)**
- Confirm audience, age bands, screening criteria
- Build moderation guide and stimulus
- Expert review to identify friction
- Synthetic AI insights to sharpen hypotheses

**Stage 2: Fieldwork (Weeks 2-3)**
- 20 virtual sessions (60 min each)
- 10 UK participants, 10 Czech participants
- Screen-share & think-aloud observation
- UK first, then Czech in native language

**Stage 3: Analysis & Synthesis (Week 4)**
- Connect usability & proposition threads
- Decision-oriented structuring
- Cross-market synthesis
- Prioritised launch recommendations

## Design Principles

1. **Clear bullseye audience** - Analyse age bands separately to avoid "dirty averages"
2. **Optimised before testing** - Expert review + AI simulation before fieldwork
3. **Don't test in vacuum** - Use competitive stimulus and best-in-class inspiration

## Key Constraints

- Small charity budget - work should be valuable but not exhaustive
- Development wraps end-January
- Launch late February
- Flag showstoppers, but prioritise feasible fixes
- Major redesign items become launch-backlog

## Deliverables Expected

- Prioritised issues ranked by impact/urgency
- Clear recommendations on proposition, onboarding, content, engagement
- Evidence (verbatims, behavioural patterns) for partner conversations
- Forward-looking direction on premium opportunities and roadmap
