## Project Overview 

### What is Money Monsters? 
A gamified financial literacy app for young people, styled after Duolingo. Users learn to "tame their money monsters" through progressive lessons covering budgeting, saving, interest, and investing fundamentals. 

**Key Features:** - Structured levels with progressive difficulty - Baseline knowledge check - Vocabulary collection mechanics - UK and Czech versions available 

**Target Audience:** - Primary: Young people (13-25) - Three distinct bands: - 
**Young Teens (13-15):** Forming habits, need strong clarity and scaffolding - 
**Older Teens (16-18):** More real-money relevance, higher scepticism - **Young Adults (18-25):** Some to full independence, increasing financial literacy and responsibility 
**Recruitment:** Recruitment Principles - Mainstream participants: typical young people, not finance enthusiasts - Skew toward need: prioritise lower financial literacy and lower income - Exclude the wealthy: remove very financially comfortable households - No prior formal training: exclude any who’ve completed structured education - Exclude finance households: no parents working in banking, accounting, FS 

### Research Objectives 
Establish product-market fit and clear launch-readiness priorities. ### Core Questions to Answer: 1. **Proposition clarity** - Do users instantly get what Money Monsters is and why it matters? 2. **Triggers & barriers** - What prompts users to try/return vs. drop off? 3. **Usability & comprehension** - Can they navigate and understand without help? 4. **Engagement & motivation** - Does gamification create momentum? 5. **Pre-launch priorities** - What to fix now vs. queue for later? 6. **Premium direction** - What could drive retention and justify premium tier?
